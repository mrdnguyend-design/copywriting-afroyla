Hey {{ person.first_name|default:'love' }},

I was at the kitchen table last night going through customer emails. George was asleep. The house was quiet. Just me and my laptop and a cup of tea that went cold two emails ago.

Most of them are quick. "Love the bag." "When's the next sale." "Can you make one in burgundy." (I'm working on it.)

Then I opened one from a woman named Yoka T.

She wrote: *"Normally I don't buy wallets. But since I buy from Afroyla, I have bought the wallet to match each bag I've purchased."*

I read that twice.

This woman has a wallet for every bag. Not one wallet. A MATCHING wallet. For each bag. That's not a customer. That's a whole system. Big Ron has a spreadsheet with backup options. Yoka has a wallet-per-bag matching protocol. My customers are running tighter operations than my warehouse.

But it was the next part that got me.

*"I love how there is a bag to truly represent US & our personality or the statement you want to make."*

To represent US.

I put my tea down. (It was already cold anyway.)

Because that's the thing. That's always been the thing. Aina means "forever" in Swahili. Huriya means "freedom." Siyanda means "we are growing." Every name on my site, I chose on purpose. For us. For the woman carrying it to know she's carrying something that was NAMED for her.

My daughter Nia walked in right then. "Mom, why are you still up?"

"Reading emails."

She looked at my screen. "You know Queen Latifah was born today, right?"

"...what?"

"Dana Owens. Found the name Latifah in a book of Arabic names when she was eight. It means delicate. She chose it herself."

Nia is twelve and knows more random facts than the internet.

But she was right. March 17th. Queen Latifah. A girl who decided at eight years old that the name she was given wasn't the name she was going to carry. So she picked one that meant something.

That's what Yoka T. was saying without saying it. The bag isn't just a bag. The name on it isn't just a name. It's the one you chose. For yourself.

Then I pulled up another email. Felicia B.

*"My niece admired one of my bags and I just had to get her one for her birthday. Because it was such a great sale, of course I had to get another for myself."*

"Of course."

I love that "of course." Like it wasn't even a decision. Her niece saw the bag. Needed it. Felicia bought two. One for the niece. One more for herself. Because when the price is right, you don't buy one. You buy one for everyone you love and then you go back for seconds.

Thursday. March 20th. 10AM. The prices are going to be right.

Wallets from $9.95. Bags from $14.95. Every name on the site. The Aina. The Huriya. The Siyanda. Every queen. Nothing held back.
