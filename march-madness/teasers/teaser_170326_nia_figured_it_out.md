Hey {{ person.first_name|default:'love' }},

I'm going to be honest with you. I've been sitting here since 5AM trying to figure out what to write you today.

Three days until March Madness. I've already told you about Big Ron's spreadsheet. I've already told you the prices. I've already told you to text AFR995E at least six times. I was staring at my screen like... what do I say TODAY that I haven't already said.

Then my daughter Nia walked in. She's twelve. She looked at me, she looked at my laptop, she looked at my cold coffee, and she goes:

"Mom, are you writing another one of those emails."

"Nia. Go eat breakfast."

"Because you've been STRESSING about this sale. You were mumbling about wallets in your sleep last night."

"I was NOT —"

"Dad heard it too. He said you said '$9.95' and then rolled over."

I don't have a defense for that.

She sat down. Grabbed my orange juice. Finished it. (She didn't pour it. I poured it. For ME. But Nia doesn't live by anyone's rules, Nia lives by Nia's rules.)

"You know what today is?" she said.

"...three days before March Madness."

"Queen Latifah's birthday."

"Oh. That's nice. Did you send her a greeting?"

"Mom, I don't have Queen Latifah's number."

"That's never stopped you before."

She rolled her eyes. Then she pulled up something on her phone and shoved it in my face.

"Did you know Queen Latifah's REAL NAME is Dana Owens?"

"Yes, Nia—"

"Her real name is DANA. And she found the name Latifah in a book when she was little. It means delicate. And she just... picked it. For herself. And then put QUEEN in front of it because... I mean... why not."

She was getting excited now. Bouncing in the chair. Then she stopped. Looked at me. Looked at my laptop. Looked at the Afroyla site open in the other tab.

"Mom."

"What."

"Mom. Your bags. The Aina. The Huriya. The Siyanda."

"Nia."

"You did the same thing. You named all your bags after queens."

"Nia, that's a COINCIDENCE."

"MOM. Did you get the idea from Queen Latifah?"

I looked at my empty juice glass. Then back at Nia.

"It was a collective inspiration."

"YOU DID."

I'm not confirming anything. But my twelve-year-old daughter, sitting at my kitchen table in pajamas with MY juice, cracked something I've never told anyone. Every bag name on my site is chosen on purpose. Aina is Yoruba for strength. Huriya is Arabic for freedom. Siyanda is Zulu for "we are growing." Names from our people. Our languages. Where the idea first started... I'm taking that one to my grave. (Nia disagrees. She's already texted three friends.)

She looked at me grinning, then goes: "So what are you going to write in the email?"

I said "I DON'T KNOW, Nia, you just derailed my entire plan."

"Write about this."

"About WHAT?"

"About me. Figuring it out. People love when you talk about me."

...she's not wrong.

A matching wallet. For every bag. This woman has a SYSTEM.

If you already own one — $9.95 gets you the matching wallet. Same queen's name. Same leather. You've been meaning to get it and this Thursday is when you do.

Thursday. March 20th. 10AM. Every queen. Every warrior. Every name on the site. Nothing held back.
