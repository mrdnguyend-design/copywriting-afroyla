"If you shaved your head tomorrow... who would you look like?"

Keisha, who designs every bag I sell, asked this at dinner Saturday night. Me, her, and Tanya, my girl since college. Wine was poured. Breadsticks were gone. Nobody was solving world problems.

Tanya was already tilting her chin up, posing. "Lupita."

(We nodded. Tanya has the cheekbones. The neck. She'd absolutely be Lupita.)

Keisha: "Grace Jones. 1985."

(The table went quiet. Then Tanya: "Yeah. I see it." Keisha smiled. She already knew.)

Then they both looked at me.

I took a sip of wine.

"...Ne-Yo."

Tanya put her fork down. Keisha choked on her Merlot. My husband George, from the other end of the table: "She's not wrong!"

GEORGE. WE WERE NOT TALKING TO YOU.

Look. I know my head. My head has TERRAIN. George once told me he counted 4 bumps. I said George, why are you COUNTING. He said "I'm an explorer, babe."

So no. I'm not shaving my head. I love my braids. That's MY thing.

* * *

But here's what happened after dinner.

We walked to the car. And I saw her.

Corner booth. Three women. Laughing the kind of laugh where nobody cares who hears.

One of them. Bald. Completely. No wig. No scarf. Nothing.

Long earrings. Red lip. And a smile that made the whole restaurant feel like HER living room.

I stopped walking.

George: "You know her?"

"No."

"Why are you staring?"

"Because she's BEAUTIFUL, George."

George looked. Quiet for a second. "She looks like one of them warriors in Wakanda Forever."

(George sometimes says things that make me forget he thinks SEO stands for 'Super Excellent Offer.')

I stood there, watching this woman laugh with her friends. And I realized why I couldn't look away.

It wasn't because she was bald.

It was because she wasn't checking the room.

She wasn't adjusting anything. Wasn't reaching for a hat. Wasn't pulling at edges. She was sitting there like the whole restaurant was invited into HER world. Not the other way around.

And nobody around her was staring out of pity. They were staring because they couldn't help it. Nothing to do with her hair or her outfit. Everything to do with the way she held her glass, the way she tipped her head back when she laughed, the way she sat like she owned every table in the room and was letting the rest of us borrow a seat.

That's a woman who decided she was beautiful. And everybody else just... agreed.

* * *

I went home. Couldn't stop thinking about her.

And somewhere around midnight, still lying there, I realized: I'd already met her. Years ago. On paper.

The woman on the Baldisa bag.

I designed her years ago. Bald. Crown on her head. Chin up. Eyes forward. No apology on her face.

I didn't know who she was for. I just knew she needed to exist.

Now I know. She's for the woman in the corner booth. She's for every woman who ever walked into a room and decided: this is MY room now.

And she's for every woman who hasn't done that yet, but felt something stir when I described it just now.
