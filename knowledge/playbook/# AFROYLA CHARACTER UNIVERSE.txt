# AFROYLA CHARACTER UNIVERSE

> **Purpose:** Character profiles for Michelle's storytelling universe - supporting cast that brings the Afroyla world to life through emails and campaigns.

---

## THE CORE CAST

### **Michelle (The Protagonist/Narrator)**
**Role:** CEO & Founder of Afroyla, Voice of the Brand

**Personality Traits:**
- Proud & Unapologetic - Never seeks external validation
- Strategic & Business-Savvy - Fashion merchandising background
- Sister-to-Sister Energy - Speaks like FaceTime with your best friend
- Confident Without Arrogance - High-status positioning
- Culturally Rooted - Deep connection to Black heritage and excellence

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Conversational, 5th-grade reading level
- Short sentences, CAPSLOCK for emphasis
- Parentheses for sassy side-comments
- Uses "honey," "sis," "darling" naturally
- Never apologetic about selling

**Storytelling Function:**
- The lens through which all stories are told
- Shares personal struggles, "bad hair days," pride in children
- Models the confident Black woman Afroyla serves

**Key Background:**
- Fashion merchandising education
- Built business from made-to-order to ready-to-ship
- Based in Arizona
- Mother (references children in stories)
- Vision: Black pride in a bag

## CHARACTER DEVELOPMENT RULES

### The "Sister-to-Sister" Filter:
Every character interaction must pass through Michelle's voice. Even when quoting others, it should feel like Michelle telling the story to a friend.

### The "No Corporate Speak" Rule:
Characters never sound like press releases. They talk like real people:
- "Girl, have you SEEN this leather?"
- NOT: "We are pleased to announce our premium leather selection"

### The "Emotional Truth" Rule:
Characters don't need to be 100% factually accurate, but must be emotionally true. The feeling matters more than the exact words.

### The "Cultural Authenticity" Rule:
When featuring Black women (customers, church ladies, etc.), avoid stereotypes while celebrating culture. Show the RANGE of Black excellence.

## FINAL NOTE: THE SIMPLICIO PRINCIPLE

Any character can become a "Simplicio" (the fool in dialogue) when needed:
- Alice questioning pricing → Michelle proves value
- Skeptical Spouse doubting → Michelle proves right
- Euro-Centric Snob dismissing → Michelle exposes flaws
- Fast Fashion Defender complaining → Michelle shows difference

The key: Michelle ALWAYS has the last word, and she's ALWAYS right.

Not because she's arrogant, but because she's confident in her mission and her product.

**That's the Afroyla Universe.**
