## SD-E2 — "IT'S LIVE" (Mass, 10:00 AM)

### 🎯 BEN'S REVIEW

**Hook Power:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ / 5
"I just hit publish" works. It's real, it's human, it's a moment. Good for mass too.

**Personality:** ⭐⭐⭐ / 5
George "do it do it do it" is a nice touch. But the middle section feels like it was copy-pasted from the Registered email without thinking about WHO is reading this.

**Sales Psychology:** ⭐⭐ / 5
Here's the big problem:
- Line: "And when they sell out, they're gone. No restocking. No coming back Monday." — **The mass list hasn't been warmed up with sell-out rules.** Registered people got 4+ emails explaining how this sale works. Mass people? This is possibly their FIRST email about March Madness. You can't start with scarcity rules when they don't even know what the event IS yet. This reads like you're warning them about something they haven't been invited to yet. Lead with the INVITATION, not the RULES.
- Line: "One customer set 4 alarms for this morning. Four. She told me she's buying 12 things." — **Mass doesn't know Katie.** This is a Registered-segment inside joke. For mass, the reader thinks: "Why does some stranger's alarm clock matter to me?" It's not FOMO for mass — it's confusion. Replace with something universally relatable: the PRICE. $9.95 is the hook for mass. That's the shocking number. Use the price the way the USPS guy used it — let the number speak.

**Line-by-line improvements:**
- Line: "And when they sell out, they're gone. No restocking. No coming back Monday."
  → Cut entirely. Replace with emphasis on WHAT this event IS and WHY $9.95 is insane.
  → Why: Mass needs the WOW first, not the fine print.

- Line: "One customer set 4 alarms for this morning. Four. She told me she's buying 12 things."
  → Replace: "Some of these wallets? They used to be $39. $42. $45. Today they're $9.95. ALL of them."
  → Why: Price shock > stranger's alarm clock. Let the numbers create FOMO.

### 🎨 DANIEL'S REVIEW

**Scene Construction:** ⭐⭐⭐ / 5
The George moment is fine but the email lacks a SCENE. For mass, you need to paint a picture of what's happening RIGHT NOW. "I'm watching inventory drop in real time" is good — expand that. Make it VISUAL.

**Emotional Pacing:** ⭐⭐⭐ / 5
The pacing rushes to the CTA without building enough desire. Mass needs: (1) WHAT is this event, (2) WHY should I care ($9.95!!), (3) WHO is already in, (4) HOW do I join (text AFR995E). Right now it goes: publish → price → rules → Katie → text. The order should be: publish → price shock → people are already moving → your turn → text.

### 📋 JOINT ASSESSMENT

**Overall Score:** ⭐⭐⭐ / 5

✅ Top 3 Strengths:
1. "I just hit publish" = universal hook
2. Double CTA (text AFR995E twice)
3. P.S. explains separate sale page — important for mass

⚠️ Top 3 Improvements:
1. Remove sell-out rules (mass hasn't been warmed up)
2. Remove Katie reference (mass doesn't know her)
3. Lead with PRICE SHOCK ($9.95 for wallets that were $39-$45) instead of scarcity rules

## SD-E2b — "The USPS Guy" (Mass, 5:45 PM)

**Hook Power:** ⭐⭐⭐ / 5
"Quick March Madness update: we have our MVP of Day 1." — This assumes mass already knows about March Madness. Some do (they got the morning email). But many might have MISSED the morning email, or skimmed it but didn't text AFR995E yet. The opening needs to RE-ESTABLISH: what is March Madness, what's the offer, how do you get in. Then the MVP story has context.
