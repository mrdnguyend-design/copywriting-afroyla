{{ person.first_name|default:'love' }},

Yesterday's email, the one where you picked 6 of my designer Keisha's 10 new bags, has a small problem this morning.

I sat down at my desk, opened the spreadsheet, and Keisha messaged me:

— Michelle, I'm having trouble picking the 6th design.

— Why...?

— Five cleared cleanly. Number ten was the clear loser. Six, seven, eight, nine — basically tied. I can't pick the sixth.

— So you want to keep yours.

— I want to keep mine.

I open the spreadsheet. She's right — the bottom four sit at the same score like they planned it.

— Keisha, most of my list is 35-plus. They know my taste. This bag isn't it.

— So ask the 25-year-olds.

I sit there.

— Let me ask them, then. Today.

— How.

— Email. They reply, or they forward to their nieces.

— ...That might actually work.

Here she is. The bag Keisha drew thinking about a twenty-seven-year-old who walks into a coffee shop and wants the room to know she walked in.

So here's the favor.

If you're between 25 and 35, reply with one word: *carry* or *skip*. One word is enough.

If you're not, forward this to your niece, your daughter, your goddaughter, the girl you used to babysit who's grown now. Ask her the same. Send back what she says.

That's it. No tracking link. No code. No "limited time." Just one word from the women this bag might actually be for.

The five winners stay locked either way. Number six is the one we're deciding together — and "we" might mean somebody in your phone contacts who's never read one of my emails before today.

Hit forward. Send it to her tonight.

Love,
Michelle
