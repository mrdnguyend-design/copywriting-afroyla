# DAILY | 010226 | Happy BHM

I was deep in that Sunday morning sleep. You know the one. The kind where you're half-dreaming, half-pretending you didn't hear your alarm.

Then — BANG.

My bedroom door flies open.

"Good morning, Mom! Can you GUESS what day it is today?"

Nia. Fully dressed. Phone in hand. Grinning like she just cracked the code on something big.

(It's 7 AM on a Sunday. My daughter has more energy than I will all day.)

"Hmm..." I squint. "February 1st?"

"And DO you know what that means?"

"Happy Black History Month, Nia?"

"YES." She bounces. "But also — can you guess WHO this is?"

She holds up her phone.



I stare at it. Squint harder.

"Nia... I genuinely have no idea."

She GASPS. Like I just said something unforgivable.

"Mom. SERIOUSLY?"

(My daughter. The teacher. Me. The student. Every single time.)

"Okay, okay — tell me," I laugh.

So she does.

She tells me that on THIS exact day — February 1st, 1978 — the U.S. Postal Service issued its very first stamp honoring a Black woman.

And that woman?

Harriet Tubman.

Moses herself. The woman who carried dozens of people out of slavery through the Underground Railroad. Who became a spy and a nurse for the Union Army. Who risked EVERYTHING — not once, not twice — but again and again — for freedom.

And nobody put her on a stamp until 1978.

Nia pauses. Looks at me. Dead serious now.

"Mom. I'm going to learn one cool Black history fact. Every. Single. Day."

My daughter decided how she's celebrating Black History Month this morning.

Not with a post. Not with a hashtag.

With one fact. Carried with her. Every single day.

And honestly? That's the most Afroyla thing I've ever heard.

Because that's exactly what we do, Sis. We CARRY it.

The history. The pride. The excellence.

Everywhere we go... in how we walk into a room, in how we hold ourselves, in what we choose to put on our arm when we walk out that door.

Today is Day 1.

Carry your crown. 👑
