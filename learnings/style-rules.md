# 📏 Style Rules (cập nhật liên tục)

> Rules dưới đây được rút ra từ user feedback qua nhiều emails.
> Đọc section này TRƯỚC khi viết bất kỳ email nào.

1. **ONE story per email.** Don't cram Karen + Marian Anderson + Nia. Save extra material for future emails.
2. **ONE throughline per email.** If you can't state the message in one sentence, it's too many messages.
3. **Michelle must be IN the story**, not just narrating from outside. Her REACTION is the emotional anchor. ("I put my phone down.")
4. **INHABIT scenes, don't summarize them.** "Black dress. Folding chairs. The kind of silence that sits on your chest." > "She went to a funeral."
5. **Don't pre-sell the story.** No "If I told you..." preambles. Either the story is good enough or it isn't.
6. **Context-free emails.** Readers may not have read previous emails. Every email must stand alone. No "Day 17" references that need backstory.
7. **Save unused material.** Cut details = future email ideas, not wasted research.
8. **Paste raw customer words.** "bc I just stop to use the restroom" > paraphrasing into clean prose. Weird spelling, "lol", emoji = undeniable proof of real person.
9. **Every scene needs a TURN.** Location ≠ scene. A scene needs a gap between expectation and reality. Add internal doubt → decision → surprise reward.
10. **Segment-specific voice.** Registered SMS = direct, urgent, Michelle selling. High Engaged SMS = personal, warm, George as bridge, Michelle inviting. Mass Email = scene-based, long-form, mission-first.
11. **Recurring characters ARE the campaign architecture.** George wasn't just comedy — his arc (noticing → attending → announcing → going silent → writing DONE) carried the emotional weight of the entire IWD sale. Build characters across emails, their silence hits harder than their words.
12. **Show the receipt.** "Not a pledge. Not a promise. Not 'a portion of proceeds.' A transfer. Done." Transparency = trust = future sales. Always show proof of mission impact.
13. **SMS = friend texting, not performing comedy.** No scripted dialogue format ("George: / Me:"). No constructed punchlines. Michelle shares her day and the joke emerges from the situation. Think group chat, not standup routine.
14. **SMS cadence = one thought per line.** Each line = one thumb-typed message. No compound sentences. Fragments OK. "lol" over forced emoji.
15. **Scarcity through conversation, not marketing.** Drop numbers casually through stories ("300 order limit and 4,000 people on the list"), not through "ACT FAST" urgency blocks.
16. **SMS jokes must be organic.** Funny situations > funny writing. "Someone asked to buy early" is naturally funny. "I'm financially unwell" is constructed comedy. The first works, the second doesn't.
17. **Em dash (—) overuse = AI tell.** Max 2-3 per email. Replace with periods, commas, parentheses, ellipses. One dramatic pause per email is fine; every other sentence is a fingerprint.
18. **Don't borrow details between emails in a series.** Each email must have its OWN scene, its OWN imagery. If E1 has a spreadsheet moment, E2 cannot have a spreadsheet moment. Same prices? Reframe differently ("$140 bags at $14.95" in E1 → "$9.95 wallets" in E2). Copy-paste = reader déjà vu.
19. **Opening = event/offer + hook in 3-5 lines.** Cold reader must know: what's being sold, when, at what price BEFORE the story starts. No "quick update" preambles. Lead with the number or the question.
20. **SMS teaser ≠ corporate announcement.** "Heyyy" > "Attention." "Reply AFR995E to learn more" > "Text to join." Caller = Michelle texting a friend, not a brand sending a blast.
21. **Real customer voices > manufactured stories for social proof emails.** Raw quotes (typos, emoji, slang preserved) are more convincing than any story you can write. Curate in escalating emotional order: comedy → enthusiasm → practical → emotional centerpiece.
22. **Product section must stay in scene.** After an emotional story, DON'T switch to "Blacknificent = That's for the girl who..." listicle format. That's a product page, not Michelle. Instead: show Michelle IN a moment with the product ("I pulled up the sketch and stared at it"). Let the story do the selling. The reader already knows what the bag means — you just spent 600 words showing them.
23. **P.S. jokes must be thematically connected.** George's P.S. should riff on the EMAIL's theme, not be a random character gag. "Can I get one that says what I needed to hear at 14?" (mirrors Toni's question) > "Not Bald, Just Aerodynamic" (disconnected bald joke). The Phyllis email's George joke worked because it was ABOUT the email's subject.
24. **No triple-And anaphora.** "And she walked... And she looked... And she didn't see..." = AI pattern. Humans don't stack three And-sentences. Restructure: merge, vary, or cut.
25. **"Polite vs quiet" reinterpretation.** When a character visits/reacts to something, give Michelle a LATER REALIZATION about what the reaction ACTUALLY meant. "I figured she was being polite" → later: "She hadn't been polite. She'd been QUIET." This is a scene TURN — Michelle's understanding shifts, and the reader shifts with her.
26. **No naked pivot (R.E.B. Framework).** Story phải Relevant + Entertaining + Brief *trước khi* product xuất hiện. "Now. Real talk." / "Speaking of which..." / "That's why I..." = naked pivot. Nếu story không tự nhiên dẫn tới product → story sai, không phải pivot sai. Fix story, không fix transition.
27. **Anti-staccato: vary paragraph length.** One-line paragraphs có impact VÌ chúng hiếm. Stack ≥3 cái liên tiếp → impact bằng 0, nhịp đọc chặt cứng. Sau 2 đoạn ngắn → một đoạn dài hơn. Rhythm = contrast.
28. **No echo writing.** Không lặp cùng cấu trúc câu ≥3 lần liên tiếp. "She walked... She looked... She didn't..." / "I put down... I picked up... I stared at..." = AI fingerprint. Vary: đổi subject, dùng verb-first fragment, dùng dialogue, dùng parenthetical.

29. **No corporate-speak character intros.** "Keisha (our Head Designer)" / "Marcus, our warehouse manager" / "X, our Y" = newsletter framing. Real Michelle introduces by name + action context: "Keisha just dropped..." Cold reader infers role from what character DOES. Warm reader knows already. Parenthetical biographical aside = AI tell + press-release voice. Same trap: "brand-new collection" / "we're producing" / "I need you to" / "stay for the story" = copywriter slipping into corporate diction. Test: would Michelle text this to a friend? If no → rewrite.

30. **Heritage references = LIVING modern reclamation, NOT ancestor relic.** When writing about cultural symbols (Adinkra, Kente, Sankofa, etc.), default frame = "what this generation does with this NOW" — tattoos, streetwear, social-currency moments, brunch-culture noticing. NOT "your grandmother's grandmother stamped on cloth in [country] [N] years ago." Grandmother metaphor = museum vibe = kills "trending/young/kinetic" energy that modern collections need. Symbols are ANCIENT but the bag/collection is RIGHT NOW. Frame Black creative class as ACTIVE owners of cultural script, not honored inheritors of artifact.

31. **Cultural beats need SCENE, not ESSAY.** Don't explain symbol meanings textbook-style. Either embed meaning IN action (Michelle Googles at midnight + discovers) OR list meanings as concrete shapes ("a heart = patience, a bird looking back = go back and get") delivered conversationally. Never use "X is what people in Y did Z years ago" — that's history-class voice. Cultural reveal must happen WITH a verb (took, covered, layered, claimed, tagged), not THROUGH passive exposition.

32. **Punchy ≠ short-stack.** AI default when going for "punchy" = stacking 4+ standalone short sentences. That's NOT punchy — that's AI-pattern fragmentation that user feels as choppy/giật cục. Earned punchiness = ONE short sentence AFTER a longer flowing setup ("Three days later, I haven't cut anything. Every time I try to land on six, I end up at eight. I want all eight in my own closet, but we can only make six. So I'm not the one picking. **You are.**"). Connect with subordinate clauses, commas + connectors ("but," "and," "every time," "trying to"), participial phrases. Style Rule #27 says vary length — this rule says DEFAULT to flowing + earn the short.

33. **Cold-reader anchor non-negotiable in opener (specifies #19).** First 3-5 lines must deliver: WHO writes / WHAT product or topic / STAKE for reader / ASK or hook. Stripping context for "straightforward" without anchoring = cold reader confusion ("ko hiểu đang viết về nội dung gì"). Even minimal openers need WHO + WHAT context within 5 lines (Apr 28 men's-line + May 06 Keisha-Adinkra both validated this). When in doubt, add ONE context anchor sentence. Test: read first 5 lines as if you've never heard of Afroyla — do you know who wrote this and what they want?

34. **Polarization line goes MID-BODY, not opener.** Ben Settle "tell haters to leave" play works best as a mid-cultural-beat filter ("If you like X, this isn't for you"), not as cold-open. Opener pulls reader IN. Mid-body filter pre-qualifies engagement quality. Position: AFTER vibe build-up, BEFORE final promise. May 06 Keisha-Adinkra placement: after "loud, tagged, claimed" cultural beat, before "walks into a room before you do" promise. 1-2 sentences max, no apology, no softening.

35. **Survey emails: early CTA pattern + 180° posture-flip when repeating mechanics.** (a) **Early CTA:** Survey emails put first CTA after opener (3-5 lines + hand-off + CTA), second CTA after story explains why. Two click-points = reader can convert at line 6 OR line 50. Apr 28 men's-line + May 06 Keisha-Adinkra both validated. (b) **Posture-flip:** When re-using a story mechanic that worked recently (Apr 24 Keisha-survey + May 06 Keisha-survey = same structural mechanic), INVERT the emotional posture rather than rebuilding the structure. Apr 24 = Michelle the JUDGE ("I don't love all of them"). May 06 = Michelle the FAN-GIRL ("I love almost all of them, want 8/10 in my closet"). Same mechanic, opposite posture = differentiation without rebuild + avoids fatigue when bank flags "3rd survey in 12 days."

36. **No self-template opener repeat.** Cross-reference last 14 days of `examples/daily/` BEFORE writing opener. If structural pattern (`[time] this morning. [place]. [prop].` / character-action-led / dialogue-led) used 2+ times → change opener structure. Apr 24 "9:04 this morning. Her sketchbook on my desk. My coffee going cold." + May 06 + May 07 v4 attempt "7:42 this morning. Kitchen table. Coffee." = template echo = AI fingerprint. Same structure twice in 14 days = retire. Test: paste your opening 3 lines next to last 5 emails' opening 3 lines — if same skeleton, rewrite. Action-led ("Keisha didn't wait. Phone went off mid-spreadsheet.") OR dialogue-led ("— Don't cut six.") OR direct-address ("Yesterday's email has a small problem.") = alternatives that don't share the time-place-prop skeleton. See `learnings/style-tics.md` for active retired patterns list.

37. **Dialogue naturalness test (read aloud).** Real text-message / Slack rhythm = fragments, interruptions, lowercase OK, multi-message bursts, typo-prone. Scripted theater = complete sentences, perfect grammar, neat turn-taking, rhetorical volleys. Marketing-deck = slogans, declarative thesis statements ("It's a winner with the right people."). For each line of dialogue: read aloud. If it sounds like a TED talk inside a quote-mark costume OR a slogan tagline — REWRITE as fragment + interruption + 1-word reply. Real Keisha: "How." (period). "...Yes." (trailing dots admit defeat). NOT: "That's because they aren't who this bag is FOR." (capitalized FOR + complete sentence + thesis = stage play). Test 2: would two coworkers actually message each other this way at 7:43 AM, or is it scripted for a reader watching?

38. **No meta-narration (writer-on-stage labeling the joke).** Forbidden phrases — copywriter exits character to label what just happened: *"That was the [whole/email/point]"* / *"He's the reason this email has a forward button"* / *"the answer to a problem I'd been wrestling with"* / *"That, weirdly, was the email"*. The reader felt the joke. The reader does not need a tour guide pointing at the joke. If the line wants to summarize-the-bit, replace with action: *"Kept walking."* / *"He's the reason I wrote this."* (vague enough — not labeling the punchline structure). Test: any sentence describing the email's CONSTRUCTION rather than the email's STORY = meta-narration. Cut.

39. **ASK section conversational, not bullet/bold-formatted.** Forbidden ASK formatting in body: **bold-prefixed conditionals** (e.g., `**If you're 25 to 35** — reply...`), bullet lists with em-dash separators, sales-page UX with colon-cascade. These format-tells signal lead-magnet checklist, not Michelle-talking. Required: flowing conditional sentences. ✅ "If you're between 25 and 35, reply with one word: *carry* or *skip*. One word is enough." ❌ "**If you're 25-35** — reply *carry* or *skip*. (More words welcome.)" The first reads as friend-explaining, the second reads as marketing UI. Test: would Michelle bold-format a sentence in a text? No → drop the bold. Italics on single keywords (*carry* / *skip*) = OK. Bold-prefixed conditional clauses = forbidden.

40. **Imperative closes carry weight.** Tepid closes forbidden: "Forward away." / "Click away." / "Take a look." / "Buy if you want." / "[verb] away" pattern signals weak permission, not invitation. Required structure: imperative verb + specific destination + soft urgency or stakes. ✅ "Hit forward. Send it to her tonight." / "Open the link. Pick one." / "Reply with one word. Today, not later." / "Read the next paragraph slowly." ❌ "Forward away." / "Reply if you want." / "Click around." Test: does the close ask the reader to DO something specific in a specific place at a specific time? If 2 of 3 missing → rewrite as full imperative.

41. **No floating metaphors (cross-version consistency).** When a metaphor PAIR is introduced (X vs Y, side door vs main door, dinner-party vs doorbuster), the pair must stay paired across iterations OR both die together. If trimming one half across versions, the other half loses meaning and floats. May 07 v3 introduced "side door / main door" pair → v7 dropped "side door" → v8-v9 floating "main door is still #6" = reader confusion ("what main door?"). At each version revision, scan: any metaphor pair from prior version where one half just got cut? Restore both or remove both. Single floating metaphors = writer-creative-language = unrecognizable to cold reader.

42. **Subjects promise feeling, not describe event.** "Keisha messaged me about #6 this morning." = describes event = low-hook (reader scans, bounces). "Help me cut a bag." = promises action + curiosity gap = high-hook. Test for every subject: does it create a curiosity gap, promise a feeling, or pose a stake? If yes → ship. If it just summarizes what's inside in one polite sentence → rewrite. Strong subject patterns: visceral imperative ("Help me cut a bag."), conflict tease ("My designer is mad at me."), specific anomaly ("Keisha won't let me cut #6."), mystery question ("Five are in. The sixth is a fight."). Weak subject patterns: descriptive event labels ("Keisha messaged me about X"), polite politely-worded summaries ("An update on our collection"), formal announcements ("Survey results inside"). Subject must work as standalone curiosity device — preview text supports it but doesn't carry it.

## 🎯 Teaser Email Checklist (for Sale Events)

> Đọc checklist này TRƯỚC khi viết bất kỳ teaser email nào cho sale.
> Rút ra từ IWD 300 Mission (5 teasers, 7+ vòng sửa email #1) và BLS/MLK campaigns.

### Cấu trúc

- [ ] **Sale-centric, not story-centric.** Context + CTA trước → story giải thích WHY. (Hot dog trước, broccoli sau.)
- [ ] **Mỗi teaser = một GÓC NHÌN khác.** Không lặp message. Ví dụ: Emotional WHY → Rational WHY → Social Proof → Mission Mechanic → Scarcity.
- [ ] **Context block viết KHÁC mỗi email.** Cùng sale info nhưng tone/angle khác. Nếu copy-paste → reader skip.
- [ ] **100% standalone.** Cold reader đọc bất kỳ email nào đầu tiên phải hiểu. Không "remember yesterday?" phụ thuộc. Callback OK chỉ khi email hoạt động khi đọc lẻ.

### Viết

- [ ] **ONE story, ONE throughline.** Nếu không nói được message trong 1 câu → quá nhiều messages. Cắt bỏ = save cho email sau.
- [ ] **Michelle phải Ở TRONG story.** Reaction của Michelle = emotional anchor. Sai: "Có phụ nữ tên Dee bị tiểu đường." Đúng: "Dee gọi lúc 11 đêm. Giọng run."
- [ ] **Scene cần TURN.** Phòng khám = location. Bác sĩ vào lúc 2:47, nói "quinoa", đưa pamphlet ảnh phụ nữ trắng ăn salad → SCENE (gap giữa expectation và reality).
- [ ] **Đừng pre-sell story.** Không "If I told you..." / "Before I tell you a story." Jump-cut vào. Story tốt không cần giới thiệu.
- [ ] **Show, don't Tell.** Không "She was crying" → "Giọng run. Thở ngắt quãng. Tiếng nước mắt lẫn trong hơi thở."

### Characters & Social Proof

- [ ] **Paste raw customer words.** Lỗi chính tả, "lol", emoji giữ nguyên = bằng chứng người thật.
- [ ] **Build recurring characters across series.** George/Tasha/Keisha → arc xuyên suốt. Im lặng mạnh hơn lời nói.
- [ ] **Ben/Daniel review cho MỖI teaser quan trọng.** Pattern: draft → review → apply 3-4 refinements → send.

### Pre-flight cuối cùng

- [ ] Đọc lại email — nếu BỎ TOÀN BỘ story, reader có hiểu sale đang bán gì không? (phải = YES)
- [ ] Đọc lại email — nếu BỎ TOÀN BỘ sale info, story có hay không? (phải = YES)
- [ ] Cả hai đều YES → email sẵn sàng.
