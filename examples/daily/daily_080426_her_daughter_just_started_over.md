# DAILY | 080426 | Her Daughter Just Started Over

**Subject:** Her daughter just started over.
**Preview:** A woman named Jovita left a review this week. I wasn't ready.

---

{{ person.first_name|default:'love' }},

When was the last time you bought somebody something... not because you had to. Not because it was their birthday. But because you knew what they'd been carrying, and you wanted them to hold something beautiful for once?

A woman named Jovita left a review this week. I need you to read what she wrote.

---

*"When I came across your purses on line I instantly fell in love and knew I had to have one, Not for me although I'd love to have one.I bought it for my daughter  she has recently left an abusive situation and she is a mother to my four precious grand babies they live 7 hours from me and that's a long drive trust!! But my daughter  doesn't even have a car so she can't visit and I work a part time job so the responsibility falls on me to help her navigate thru this life's storm.so when I saw this purse I knew she had to have it.i wish I could present her with both the hang bag and wallet but I can't  afford it just yet but I will some day until then I can't wait to gift her this she will feel inpowered and hopefully bring to her mind what a wonderful  strong mother and child she is to me...thank you from my soul"*

---

I read it twice.

The first time, I read the words. The second time, I read the mother.

She can't fix what happened. She can't undo it. She can't even drive there.

But she can wrap a bag.

That's what mothers do when the storm is too big to stop. They find one small thing and put it in your hands. Not because it fixes anything. Because it says what they can't say out loud: *I'm still here. I see you. And I will never stop reaching for you.*

Jovita isn't unusual. I've been reading your emails, your reviews, your DMs at 2 in the morning for years now, and the story is always the same. A woman who has less than she needs, spending what she has on someone she loves more than herself.

You know what nobody tells you about running a handbag store? It's not the leather you remember at the end of the day. It's these moments. A daughter buying her mama's first luxury bag. A woman treating herself after chemo. A pastor named Dentise, 73, on a tight budget, who called me "sister."

The world is loud right now. But these stories are quiet. And real. And they remind me that life is full of small beautiful things if you know where to look.

---

Jovita, if you're reading this.

I hope your daughter opens that box and goes quiet. I hope she calls you and neither of you says a word. Just breathing. That's what love sounds like when it's too big for words.

We all deserve a better life.
