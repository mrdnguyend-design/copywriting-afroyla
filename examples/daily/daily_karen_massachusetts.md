# DAILY | 170226 | Karen from Massachusetts

I was reading reviews last night. 11 PM. George asleep. House quiet.

Most reviews make me smile. Some make me cry. This one made me wake George up.

"George. GEORGE. Read this."

He squinted at my phone. Read it. Looked at me.

"Is this real?"

It's real. I'm going to paste her exact words because I couldn't make this up if I tried.

* * *

Karen from Massachusetts bought two bags on Juneteenth last year. Her sister-in-law saw them and bought FOUR.

Then the trouble started.

Karen went to a funeral. She told me she almost didn't wear the bag. A funeral — maybe it was too much. Too bold. She almost switched it out at the door.

She didn't.

And across the room, a woman she'd never met looked up. Same bag. Neither one said a word. In Karen's words: *"we shared a moment of connection over the bag."*

Two strangers. One room full of grief. And something on their arm that said — I see you.

* * *

Then Karen pulled into a car dealership. Not to buy a car.

I'll let her tell it:

*"No it was not a sales tactic bc I just stop to use the restroom."*

She didn't even make it to the bathroom.

The sales rep stopped her. Karen wrote: *"what a gorgeous bag she said… that I was the fourth person came in that same day with these beautiful bags."*

Fourth. At a Dodge dealership. On a bathroom break.

I read that line three times. Turned to George. "FOUR women. Same dealership. Same DAY."

George, half-asleep: "Babe. You're running a gang."

* * *

Then she went to work training. The presenter stopped mid-slide and asked if she could use Karen's bag as a positive affirmation for the room.

Her. Bag. Became. A. Teaching. Tool.

I put my phone down.

Karen ended her review with this: *"Love the quality, love the style and I carry them with pride every where including church. Bye to the other really expensive bags that I used to purchase. God bless you Afroyla ❤️🌺"*

That's Karen.

She wasn't trying to start a movement. She was trying to use a bathroom. And she walked into rooms carrying something so real that people couldn't help it — they stopped, they connected, they FELT something.

And real is loud, Sis. Even when you're quiet. 👑
