"Michelle?"

I jump.

George is standing in the doorway at 2:43 AM, squinting at me like I've lost my mind.

"Why are you awake?" he asks.

I'm at the kitchen table in my robe, laptop open, staring at a button that says "Complete Donation."

"I couldn't sleep," I say.

"So you decided to... work?" He walks closer, scratching his head. "At 2 in the morning?"

"2:43," I correct him.

He looks at the screen. Sees the Black Girls Code donation page.

"Wait. Did we hit 200?"

I nod.

His face breaks into a grin. "Babe! That's amazing!"

He leans over my shoulder, reading the screen.

"You've been stressing about this all weekend," he says. "Just click the button."

So I do.

I click "Complete Donation."

The screen refreshes.

And then this pops up:

Receipt #1294-0188.
Amount: $2,000.
Date: Jan 20, 2026, 2:43:23 AM.

Done.

A few seconds later, another email arrives—the thank you letter from Cristina, their CEO:

"Thanks for your generous gift of $2,000 to Black Girls Code. Your support helps answer one powerful question: Who's coding our future? Because of you, the answer is clear: BGC girls are."

George reads it over my shoulder.

"See?" he says. "You did good."

And you know what?

He's right.

WE did good.

George goes back to bed.

I stay up.

Not because I'm stressed anymore.

But because now I'm thinking about what's NEXT.
