## Notes

- **"The USPS guy just bought a wallet."** — Subject line = curiosity hook. Not salesy. Not "$9.95 SALE!!!" It's a story you want to read.
- **The dialogue IS the sales copy.** "$9.95." → "You're lying." → "I'm not lying." — This exchange communicates value better than any bullet point. The USPS guy's disbelief = reader's own reaction.
- **Marcus stare.** "That's what Marcus does. He stares. It's unsettling but effective." — Quick character beat. Doesn't slow the story. Adds personality.
- **Kadia wallet named.** The reader will now want to check if Kadia is still in stock. Product desire through story, not through a catalog listing.
- **"Your husband just did something RIGHT."** — Speaks to the wives in the audience. If the USPS guy's wife is on the list = hilarious. If not = relatable.
- **Transition from story to CTA.** "If the guy who delivers our packages couldn't walk past these prices?" — The story IS the argument. No need to add a hard sell.
- **P.S.** = SMS new-drops CTA. Same as SD-E1. Secondary ask, tucked away.
- **~350 words.** Longer than the SMS but still concise for email. The story fills the space — not padding.
