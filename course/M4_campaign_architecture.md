## 4.1 Campaign Anatomy — 4 Phases

Every multi-day sale follows 4 phases:

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╗
║ Phase 1: TEASER     │ 7-10 days  │ Build anticipation       ║
║                     │            │ Drive SMS registration    ║
╠══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╣
║ Phase 2: WARMUP     │ 3-5 days   │ Insider content           ║
║                     │            │ Game plan + screenshot     ║
╠══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╣
║ Phase 3: SALE DAY   │ 2-3 days   │ Real-time chaos + urgency ║
║                     │            │ Segment-specific emails    ║
╠══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╣
║ Phase 4: POST-SALE  │ 1 day      │ Recap, receipt proof       ║
║                     │            │ Gratitude + next teaser    ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

> **Note:** Not all campaigns use all 4 phases. MLK = Teaser + Sale only. BLS = Teaser + Sale. March Madness = all 4 phases (most complete).

## 4.3 Teaser Email Sequence — The Blueprint

### The Teaser Checklist

> Đọc này TRƯỚC khi viết bất kỳ teaser nào. Rút từ IWD (5 teasers, 7+ vòng sửa email #1) và BLS/MLK campaigns.

#### Structure

- [ ] **Sale-centric, not story-centric** — Context + CTA trước → story giải thích WHY
- [ ] **Mỗi teaser = một GÓC NHÌN khác** — Không lặp message
- [ ] **Context block viết KHÁC mỗi email** — Cùng sale info, khác tone/angle
- [ ] **100% standalone** — Cold reader đọc bất kỳ email nào đầu tiên phải hiểu

#### Writing

- [ ] **ONE story, ONE throughline** — Một câu summary test
- [ ] **Michelle phải Ở TRONG story** — Reaction = emotional anchor
- [ ] **Scene cần TURN** — Gap between expectation vs reality
- [ ] **Đừng pre-sell story** — Jump-cut vào scene

#### Pre-flight

- [ ] Bỏ TOÀN BỘ story → reader hiểu sale? = YES
- [ ] Bỏ TOÀN BỘ sale info → story hay? = YES
- [ ] Cả hai YES → sẵn sàng gửi

### Information Revelation Strategy — "Layer by Layer"

Pattern chung cho teaser sequence (mỗi email reveal thêm 1 layer):

```
Layer 1: ANNOUNCE  — Tên sale + dates + shock price + "it's coming"
Layer 2: VALIDATE  — Customer voice / bug-as-content / double touch
Layer 3: EXPLAIN   — Mechanics, how it works, analogy/metaphor
Layer 4: PROVE     — Social proof numbers, beta test, testimonials
Layer 5: STRATEGIZE — Two approaches, game plan, FAQ
Layer 6: DEADLINE  — VIP closes, last call, final prep
Layer 7: LIVE      — Real-time chaos, FOMO for non-registrants
```

Mỗi teaser dùng 1 angle khác:

### Social Proof Escalation

Build registration numbers across the sequence:

```
Email 1: (no number)
Email 2: 124
Email 3: 300+
Email 4: (urgency reminder)
Email 5: 1,200+
Email 6: LIVE → orders coming in
```

> **Rule:** Never lie about numbers. Always round down. Real numbers = trust.

### Dual-Stream: Mass + Registered

| Day | 📢 MASS | 🔒 REGISTERED |
|-----|---------|--------------|
| Day 1 | Announce sale | Fix/insider detail |
| Day 2 | Win tiers + social proof | Objection handling FAQ |
| Day 3 | Mechanic via metaphor | — |
| Day 4 | Scarcity (inventory) | Beta test / group chat |
| Day 5 | Strategy guide | — |
| Day 6 | LAST CALL (VIP closes) | Full schedule + game plan |
| Day 7 | LIVE FOMO | Sale link + insider access |

> **Pattern:** Mass = drive registrations. Registered = nurture + insider content. Never identical.

## 4.4 Warmup Emails (Registered Segment)

### Pattern (from March Madness)

| # | Days Before | Content | Voice |
|---|-------------|---------|-------|
| WE1 | -5 | Insider preview — "You're in. Here's what's coming." | Welcoming, inclusive |
| WE2 | -3 | Browse tonight — product preview + "pick your bags now" | Practical, helpful |
| WE3 | -2 | Screenshot leak — "LIVE from the Sale Page" | Behind-the-scenes, proof |
| WE4 | -1 | Game day prep — final checklist + timing | Urgent, actionable |

### Why Warmup Exists (vs. MLK/BLS)

MLK and BLS ran teaser-only → registered received general teasers.
March Madness added a separate warmup track because:

1. **FB Ads registrants** only received WE1 and didn't know the brand
2. **Warmup reduces sale-day friction** — they've already picked products
3. **Screenshot leak builds proof** — "this is REAL"
4. **Game plan reduces overwhelm** — exact times, exact strategy

## 4.5 Sale-Day Operations

### Email Cadence (3-Day Sale)

```
Day 1:
├── 10:00 AM  →  SD-E1 (Registered): "IT'S LIVE — Your Link"
├── 10:00 AM  →  SD-E2 (Mass): Tasha chaos — FOMO
└── 5:45 PM   →  SD-E1b/E2b: Second email (afternoon update)

Day 2:
├── Morning   →  SD-E3 (Registered): Day 2 update
├── Morning   →  SD-E4 (Mass): Day 2 FOMO
└── Afternoon →  APOLOGY email (if oversold/incident occurs)

Day 3:
├── Morning   →  SD-E5 (Registered): "Last 24 Hours"
├── Morning   →  SD-E6 (Mass): "Closing Tonight"
└── Evening   →  SD-E7 (Mass): Final call
```

### SMS Cadence (Registered — 3-Day Sale)

```
Day 1: 10AM (launch) → 2PM (mid-day) → 7PM (evening)
Day 2: 10AM (Day 2 link) → 6PM ("24 hours left")
Day 3: 10AM (final day) → 6PM ("6 hours") → 9PM ("3 hours")
```

### Sale-Day Content Elements

| Element | What to include | Example |
|---------|----------------|---------|
| **Real-time chaos** | Tasha yelling cities, Marcus packing | "HOUSTON. $9.95 NAIJA. GONE." |
| **George in war room** | He shows up uninvited | "George called. Heard Tasha. Hung up." |
| **Sold-out updates** | Specific products gone | "Sandra's Thundara — gone in 8 min" |
| **Affordable entry** | Mini wallet / accessories for latecomers | "You don't need handbag money" |
| **Product rotation** | Premium → mid → affordable → everything | Match time of day to product tier |
| **Competitive context** | How THIS sale differs | "23 IWD emails in your inbox. Not one did anything." |

### Character Rotation Across Sale Days

```
Day 1: Tasha (war room narrator) + George (background comedy)
Day 2: Alice (breathing exercises) + Marcus (non-verbal)
Day 3: George arc resolution + Keisha (ceiling-staring) + ALL characters reunion
```

### The Apology Playbook

If something goes wrong during sale (oversold, site crash, keyword error):

1. **Own it immediately** — "I owe you an apology"
2. **Tell the story of what happened** — behind-the-scenes scene
3. **Fix + compensate** — what you're doing about it
4. **Turn bug into content** — BLS keyword change → extra touchpoint

> **Reference:** March Madness apology email (`saleday_210326_apology_oversold.md`) and BLS keyword fix (`024_Correct_Keyword.html`)

## 4.6 Post-Sale Recap Email

### Framework (from IWD — graded A+ by Ben/Daniel)

```
1. CALLBACK OPENING — echo from early teaser
   "What happened to your $5?"

2. RECEIPT AS HERO — proof, not promises
   "Not a pledge. Not a promise. Not 'a portion of proceeds.'
    A transfer. Done."

3. WAREHOUSE SCENE — real-time final count
   298... 299... 300. George writes DONE on the whiteboard.

4. CHARACTER REUNION — name customers and team
   Sandra. Paige. "Thank you by name."

5. GRATITUDE — end on legacy, not product
   "I'm going to sit down. We finished it."

6. P.S. — BOGO or next sale teaser
   Tissue paper BOGO offer (soft, non-pushy)
```

### Key Principles

- **Show the receipt** — actual donation screenshots or transfer confirmations
- **"Transfer. Done."** — strongest 2 words. Not pledge, promise, hope.
- **Character reunion** — bring back characters from the teaser series
- **George's silence** is the emotional climax, not a punchline
- **Separate cry lines** — "I cried a little" on its own line
- **Gratitude reorder** — end on LEGACY, not on the brand

## 4.7 Content Volume Planning

### Templates by Campaign Size

| Size | Emails | SMS | Other | Total | Timeline |
|------|--------|-----|-------|-------|----------|
| **Small** (MLK-level) | 8 | 4-6 | Landing page | ~14 | 10 days |
| **Medium** (BLS-level) | 13 | 8-10 | Landing page | ~23 | 14 days |
| **Large** (MM/IWD-level) | 19 | 20+ | LP + Collection | ~39 | 21 days |

### Production Schedule Template

```
Day -14: Content plan V1 (calendar view)
Day -10: Start teaser writing (2-3 pieces/day)
Day -7:  Teasers finished, start warmup writing
Day -5:  Warmup emails done, SMS warm-up done
Day -3:  Landing page + collection setup
Day -1:  Sale-day templates + final pre-flight
Day 0-3: Real-time sale-day content (adapt to actual numbers)
Day +1:  Post-sale recap
Day +3:  Kaizen — update learnings.md
```

### Daily Workload Rating

| Pieces/day | Rating | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| 1-3 | 🟢 Light | Normal pace |
| 4-5 | 🟡 Medium | Push day |
| 6+ | 🔴 Heavy | Clear calendar, sale-day templates = lighter than looks |

### Concept Changes from Plan (Expect This)

> "E2 ban đầu = 'Daily + teaser tie-in' (generic) → thành George/NCAA naming story"

Plan V1 is never final. Expect 20-30% of concepts to change during writing. This is NORMAL. Save unused concepts for future campaigns.

## 4.8 Campaign Planning Checklist

### Pre-Campaign (Day -30 to -14)

- [ ] Define sale mechanic (fixed discount? gamified? elimination?)
- [ ] Define scarcity lever (order limit? inventory? time?)
- [ ] Define mission/reason (charitable? business? seasonal?)
- [ ] Choose SMS keyword
- [ ] Build landing page concept
- [ ] Create content calendar (email + SMS + segments)
- [ ] Estimate content volume

### Teaser Phase (Day -10 to -1)

- [ ] Write 1 teaser/day (different angle each)
- [ ] Track social proof numbers (real registrations)
- [ ] Run Ben/Daniel review on critical teasers
- [ ] Build information revelation ladder (layer by layer)
- [ ] Last call email ready for registration deadline

### Sale Day (Day 0 to +3)

- [ ] Sale-day email templates (Registered + Mass)
- [ ] SMS cadence planned (10AM / 2PM / 7PM)
- [ ] Apology email pre-drafted (just in case)
- [ ] Character assignment per day
- [ ] Product rotation per SMS

### Post-Sale (Day +1 to +3)

- [ ] Recap email with proof/receipt
- [ ] BOGO or next-sale P.S.
- [ ] Update learnings.md with post-mortem
- [ ] Save unused concepts for future
