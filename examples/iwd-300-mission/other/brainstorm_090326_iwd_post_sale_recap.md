# Mar 09 — Post-Sale Recap Email Brainstorm

## 📋 Context

- **Sale result:** 300 orders in 3 days (Mar 6-8). Mission COMPLETE.
- **Total raised:** $2,500 toward BWHI's CYL2 Program
- **Donation receipt:** Screenshot included in email — proof of $2,500 transfer
- **Segment:** Mass (everyone — buyers AND non-buyers)
- **Send:** Mar 09 (Sunday morning — the day after IWD)
- **CYL2 detail:** 12-month culturally tailored diabetes prevention coaching program. 6 months of group + 1-on-1 coaching. Coaches who look like you, eat like you.

---

## 🔬 Research Insights

- **75% of donors** look for transparency/proof before giving. The receipt screenshot IS the proof.
- **Best post-campaign emails** pair stats with stories — not just "we raised $X" but "here's what $X does"
- **Gratitude first** — the thank you should come before ANY commercial messaging
- **No selling.** This email is a celebration, not a pitch. Zero commercial CTA. Trust-building email.

---

## 💡 IDEAS

### A) "We did it. Here's the receipt."

**Hook:** Simple. Direct. The receipt is the hero.

- Open: "The 300 Mission is complete. 300 orders. Three days. $2,500 toward diabetes prevention coaching."
- Michelle's reaction: describe the moment the 300th order came in
- "I said I would show you where the money goes. Here's the receipt." → Screenshot of $2,500 donation to BWHI
- Explain what $2,500 does in the CYL2 program — coaching sessions, how many women it helps
- Thank every single person — buyers AND followers who spread the word
- George gets the last word

### B) "300."

**Hook:** One number. Full circle from the teasers.

- The campaign started with "The 300 Mission" as a concept nobody understood. Today it's a completed number.
- "Three weeks ago I told you about a mission. 300 orders. $5 each. Something bigger than a sale."
- "Yesterday at [TIME], order #300 came in. From [STATE]. A [PRODUCT]."
- "Here's what your $5 became." → Receipt screenshot
- Break down what $2,500 funds in the CYL2 program
- Thank you

### C) "What happened to your $5. The final answer."

**Hook:** Ultimate callback to Teaser #2 ("What happened to your $5?")

- Full arc: "A few weeks ago, I asked you what happened to your $5. I've been answering that question for three days straight. Here's the final answer."
- "300 orders. 300 × $5. Look:" → Receipt screenshot showing $2,500 to BWHI
- Explain what the program does. This time, not to sell — to close the loop.
- "You asked where the money goes. Now you can see it."

### D) "George had one more thing to say"

**Hook:** George-led closer to the entire campaign arc.

- Scene: Sunday morning. Sale is over. Michelle and George in the kitchen. Quiet.
- George says something genuine — maybe the first fully sincere thing he's said all campaign
- "He looked at the receipt. $2,500. And he said: 'Michelle. That's real. That's not a sale. That's real.'"
- Michelle shows the receipt → screenshot
- Then the thank you

### E) "Dear 300"

**Hook:** A letter. To the 300 women.

- Addressed to every woman who ordered. If you're reading this and you ordered — this is for you.
- "You are one of the 300. That means your $5 is in this number." → Receipt screenshot
- "Here's what your $5 is doing right now." → CYL2 breakdown
- If you're reading this and you DIDN'T order — "you spread the word. You shared the texts. You told your friends. That counts too."
- George P.S.: closing bit

## 🔥 RECOMMENDED STRUCTURE

**Best combo: C opening (callback) + A body (receipt as hero) + D/E emotional close**

### Proposed structure

1. **Opening** — "What happened to your $5? Here's the final answer." (callback to teaser series)
2. **The number** — "300 orders. Three days. $2,500."
3. **The receipt** — [SCREENSHOT] — "I said I would show you. Here it is."
4. **What $2,500 does** — CYL2 program impact: coaching sessions funded, women helped, 58% risk reduction
5. **The moment it happened** — Describe order #300 coming in. Michelle's reaction. George.
6. **Thank you** — To buyers AND everyone who followed along
7. **George P.S.** — The final George moment. Something real.

### Key rules

- ❌ **No selling.** Zero commercial CTA. This is a gratitude email.
- ✅ **Receipt screenshot is THE centerpiece.** Transparency = trust = future sales.
- ✅ **Thank non-buyers too.** "You shared the text. You told your friend. That counts."
- ✅ **CYL2 breakdown.** Show EXACTLY what the money funds. Make it tangible.
- ✅ **Close the character arc.** George gets one last genuine moment.
