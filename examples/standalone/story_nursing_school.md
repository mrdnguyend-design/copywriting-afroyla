# STORY | 110126 | NURSING SCHOOL

My phone buzzes.

My sister's name.

I make that face—you know the one—because when she texts at 7:12 PM on a Tuesday, something BIG is happening.

Me: "Hey… you okay?"

Her: "I started nursing school this week."

Me: "Okay wait—like… you started started?"

Her: "Yes. Started started."

Me: "At 42?"

Her: "At 42."

I swear I could hear her smiling through the phone… and also hear her fear doing jumping jacks in the background.

Then she starts reading me her schedule like she's confessing a crime:

"It's 15 months. Accelerated. Five days a week. Plus I work 8 to 12 hour shifts on weekends."

I'm holding my coffee with two hands now. For safety.

Me: "So… when do you sleep?"

Her: "That's funny."

Me: "No, I'm serious."

Her: "I know. That's why it's funny."

She gets quiet.

"I'm terrified. I'm excited. I'm ready… but I'm so not ready. Please send your positive thoughts my way."

I'm typing back something sweet when autocorrect strikes:

"You got this" becomes "You goat this."

Her: "Did you just call me a goat?"

Me: "YES. A STRONG GOAT. A NURSING GOAT."

* * *

This morning I pulled the Kadia off my shelf, wrapped it up, and sent it to her.

No card. No explanation.

She'll know why.

If you also want a little motivation, see the bag here.

Love,
Michelle

P.S. At 42, she's the oldest in her cohort. Some 22-year-old in Lululemon is probably going to ask if she's the professor. I hope she says yes.
