## SD-E1 — "IT'S LIVE" (10:00 AM)

### 🟢 What's Working

> **Ben:** The opening is strong. "I just hit publish. My hand was shaking." — that's a real moment. Not a marketing moment. A HUMAN moment. The reader feels like they're standing next to Michelle when it happens. Five sentences before the first link — that's discipline.
>
> **Daniel:** The Katie callback is excellent. One line. "Katie's 4 alarms just went off." Registered people know EXACTLY who Katie is. That's a callback that works as FOMO without being a sales push. And the George "do it do it do it" — quick, funny, doesn't slow anything down. Good.

### 🟡 What Needs Work

> **Ben:** THE OFFER IS BURIED. "Wallets from $9.95. Bags from $14.95." — this is ONE line, AFTER the first link. The reader might click the link before they even see the price. That's fine for some emails but this is a LAUNCH email. The price IS the hook. $9.95 wallets and bags need to hit them BEFORE the link, not after.
>
> **Daniel:** Also — "Bags from $14.95." The user said the offer is $9.95 for bags AND wallets. If that's the case, make it one clean number: **"Wallets and bags from $9.95."** One price. One shock. That's cleaner than two price points, especially in a launch email where you have 3 seconds of attention.
>
> **Ben:** The "Here's what you need to remember" section... I'd cut it. You're telling them what they ALREADY know. WE4 covered the sell-out rule. The "I already told you all of that yesterday" line is good self-awareness, but then why did you just tell them again? Either cut the sellout rule entirely OR make it sharp and fast: "Sell out = gone. You know the drill."
>
> **Daniel:** "That's the Madness." — I actually love this. Keep. But tighten the section around it. Right now it's padded.

### ✏️ Suggested Rewrite

> Remove "bags from $14.95" — replace with unified "$9.95 bags & wallets" pricing. Move pricing BEFORE first link. Tighten the middle section. Keep George, Katie, P.S.

## SD-E1b — "The USPS Guy" (5:45 PM)

### 🟢 What's Working

> **Ben:** This is one of the best sale day emails I've seen from this brand. The subject line — "The USPS guy just bought a wallet." — is PERFECT. Not salesy. Not "$9.95 SALE." It's a story you want to read. Click-through should be strong.
>
> **Daniel:** The dialogue does ALL the work. "$9.95" → "You're lying" → "I'm not lying" — THAT exchange communicates value better than any bullet point, any bold text, any "LIMITED TIME" stamp. The USPS guy's disbelief IS the sales copy. Michelle doesn't need to explain why $9.95 is good. The USPS guy already did it for her.
>
> **Ben:** And the punchline — "drove IN to pick up shipments and drove OUT carrying his own purchase" — that's the closer. The structure is sound: story → bridge → CTA. Clean arc.

### 🟡 What Needs Work

> **Daniel:** Same pricing issue. "The bags at $14.95" at the bottom — if bags are also from $9.95, fix it. And this is where the offer clarity matters most. The USPS guy story sells the WALLET at $9.95 beautifully through dialogue. But at the bottom, Michelle quietly mentions bags at a different price. The reader's brain goes: "Wait, are the bags different?" If they're the same $9.95, say so. "Wallets from $9.95. Bags from $9.95. I know. I KNOW."
>
> **Ben:** The transition from story to CTA is good but could be SHARPER. "Look — if the guy who delivers our packages couldn't walk past these prices?" — that's a solid bridge sentence. But then it goes into a block of text: "190+ orders... page is thinner... Some wallet designs... bags at $14.95..." That's four fact-points in a row. Pick TWO. Maximum. The email just told you a beautiful story — don't dump a data paragraph on top of it.
>
> **Daniel:** I'd restructure the CTA section:
> - One line of urgency ("190+ orders. Page is running thin.")
> - One line of offer reminder ("Wallets and bags from $9.95. I know.")
> - Link.
> - Done. Don't linger. The story already did the work.
>
> **Ben:** Last thing — "I don't know his wife's name but ma'am — if you're reading this — your husband just did something RIGHT." This is GOLD. Don't touch it. It makes every woman think of her own husband and whether HE would be that smart.
>
> **Daniel:** Agreed. That line stays. And I'd actually move it AFTER the punchline ("drove IN... drove OUT...") for maximum impact. Right now it's positioned well. Just make sure nothing else gets between that line and the CTA.

### ✏️ Suggested Rewrite for CTA Section

Change the bottom section from the current bulky version to:

```
Look — if the guy who delivers our packages couldn't walk past these prices?

190+ orders today. The page is running thin.

Wallets and bags from $9.95. Yeah. I know.

👉 SHOP MARCH MADNESS

— Michelle
```
