{{ person.first_name|default:'love' }},

Happy Mother's Day.

That's the email. That's the whole thing — except I want to say it a little wider than the card aisle says it.

Today belongs to the women who gave birth, yes.

And to the godmothers. The aunts who raised you when nobody else did. The older cousins who became mothers without ever being given the title.

To the friend who showed up at 2 AM the year your world fell apart and stayed until morning.

To the stepmothers who chose you. The grandmothers raising grandchildren. The women who decided not to have kids and poured into other people's anyway.

And to the woman who mothered herself because nobody else did.

To her especially.

If you're carrying today alone — because mom passed, or doesn't speak to you, or never spoke to you at all — today is still your day. The grief and the day are not enemies. They sit on the couch together.

Whatever shape your today takes, I'm glad you let me into your inbox while it's happening.

That's not a small thing.

Happy Mother's Day.

Love,
Michelle

**P.S.** If today's moved you to pick something out (for yourself, or for the woman in your life you didn't get to give a card to yet), [the storefront's open](https://afroyla.com). The one your eye keeps going back to is usually the right one.
