# Universe of Afroyla — Cast & World Bible

> **Purpose:** Reference guide for every character, location, and running gag in the Afroyla email universe. Use this to maintain consistency when writing new emails.

---

## 🎭 THE CORE CAST

### Michelle — The Narrator (CEO & Founder)
**Role:** 1st-person narrator of every email. The audience's best friend who happens to run a handbag brand.

| Trait | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Voice** | Warm, direct, emotionally intelligent. Never preachy. |
| **Sign-off** | "Love, Michelle" or "With love, Michelle" |
| **From line** | "Michelle @ Afroyla" (michelle@afroyla.com) |
| **Greeting** | `{{ person.first_name\|default:'love' }}` — uses "love" as default |
| **Relationship to reader** | "Sis" — the reader is family, not a customer |
| **Key tension** | Visionary CEO vs. practical business owner — she WANTS to give everything away, her team keeps her grounded |

**How she talks:**
- Uses "I" constantly — everything filtered through her experience
- Specific timestamps: "9:47 PM", "2:43 AM", "this morning at 6:52 AM"
- Parenthetical asides for inner thoughts: *(This is going to sound dramatic, but stay with me.)*
- Vulnerable but never weak: admits fear, uncertainty — then acts anyway

**Recurring behaviors:**
- Coffee in hand (almost every email references coffee)
- Scrolling phone/IG at odd hours
- Sitting in her office when people burst in
- Staring at screens in disbelief during sale days

### George — The Husband (Comic Relief)
**Role:** Michelle's husband. Does NOT work at Afroyla. His main contribution is "asking when's dinner" and occasionally holding a bag for product photos. **The breakout comedy character.**

| Trait | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Defining line** | "George is my husband. George does NOT work here." |
| **Hair status** | Bald 💀 (key to many jokes) |
| **Where he appears** | Primarily P.S./P.P.S. sections. Sometimes enters the main story during sale events. |
| **Running gag** | He keeps showing up to "help" and getting lovingly rejected |

**Greatest hits:**
- "George heard Tamara's story and said, 'I would look GREAT with natural hair.' George is bald. 💀"
- "George just walked by and said, 'You're doing WHAT with the prices?'"
- "George just asked Alice if he can 'approve' the Day 3 plan. Alice said 'George, I will call security.' He thinks she's joking. She is not joking."
- "George just looked at the sale page and said 'Michelle, that Thundara is $19.95. We PAID more than that for the hardware alone.' I said 'George. Go hold someone else's coffee.' 😂"
- "George just asked me what 'SEO' stands for. I said 'Search Engine Optimization.' He said 'I thought it stood for Super Excellent Offer.' Keisha walked out of the room. I heard her SCREAMING in the hallway."
- "George walks in... coffee in hand, like he's reporting for duty" → pulls up a CHAIR → starts making SUGGESTIONS → "George. We don't sell shoes." → "George. Go home."
- "George did not leave the office. George pulled up a CHAIR. George started making SUGGESTIONS."
- "(George if you're reading this, STOP.)"

**George's narrative function:**
1. **Tension breaker** after emotional stories
2. **Relatability glue** — readers know a "George"
3. **Sale mechanic credit** — his ideas (like the $9.95 backpacks) sometimes ARE genius... but don't tell him
4. **Humanizer** — makes the brand feel like a family, not a company

**Writing George rules:**
- His dialogue is always slightly off/oblivious
- Michelle's responses to him are deadpan
- He is NEVER mean or dismissed cruelly — it's love disguised as exasperation
- His best ideas are acknowledged...privately: "Don't tell him. 💜"
- Physical comedy: squinting, holding coffee, reporting for duty, walks in at wrong time

### Nia — The Daughter (Wisdom & Heart)
**Role:** Michelle's daughter, approximately 10-11 (5th grade). The moral compass of the brand. Delivers daily Black history facts, asks piercing questions, and sees the world with startling clarity.

| Trait | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Age/Grade** | 5th grader |
| **Defining trait** | Self-imposed BHM challenge (daily Black history facts in February) |
| **Where she appears** | P.S. sections (facts), main stories about family life |
| **Dream** | Wants to run a tech company; interested in Black Girls Code |
| **Name meaning** | "Nia" = Purpose in Swahili (Kwanzaa principle) |

**Nia's recurring behaviors:**
- Ambushes Michelle before coffee with facts: "My daughter stays teaching me things before I've had my coffee. ☕"
- "Fully dressed. Phone in hand. Grinning like she just cracked the code on something big."
- Reads from her phone — historical quotes, facts, connections
- Drops wisdom at dinner: "Three nights ago, I'm passing mashed potatoes when Nia drops a folded piece of paper next to her plate."
- Stares down adults with devastating calm

**Greatest hits:**
- "They showed up anyway, Mom." (re: Tuskegee Airmen)
- On George's laptop privileges: "No. You lost laptop privileges when you told my business without permission."
- To Michelle: "And Mom? Tell your cousin Afroyla is NOT Etsy."
- "Mom. Are you okay?" (driving to church, reads Michelle's stress)
- "Nia's already grabbed George's phone, scrolling. 'Black Girls Code. Look—'"

**Nia's narrative function:**
1. **Historical anchor** — connects daily emails to Black history/culture
2. **Emotional catalyst** — her questions force Michelle to reflect
3. **Brand mission embodied** — she's the "future" Afroyla is building for
4. **The WHY** — "This is about making sure when Nia grows up, she doesn't have to fight just to get a seat at the table."

### Alice — The Accountant / Operations Manager (Voice of Reason)
**Role:** Michelle's "numbers girl." The one who walks in with thick folders and spreadsheet concerns. Represents the practical/financial side of running the business.

| Trait | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Title** | Accountant (also referenced as operations) |
| **Defining line** | "Michelle, are you SURE about this?" |
| **Personality** | Cautious, spreadsheet-obsessed, quietly protective |
| **Character arc** | Starts worried → gets won over → becomes biggest supporter |

**How Alice enters stories:**
- Walks into Michelle's office "holding a thick folder"
- Texts/Slacks Michelle at key moments
- Closes laptop "like she's bracing for impact"
- Picks up her pen and starts scribbling numbers on the edge of her spreadsheet

**Greatest hits:**
- "FOUR HUNDRED WOMEN voted on bags that don't exist yet. Do you understand what this MEANS?"
- "If I broke that, Alice — my accountant — would drag me straight into Spreadsheet Jail. 😭"
- "Can you tell them to stop emailing me asking which tier we're on?" / "Alice, that means they're paying attention." / "I know, but I've answered that question like 40 times today."
- "Alice is in the corner doing breathing exercises. I wish I was joking. She's got her eyes closed and she's going 'in... two... three... out... two... three...'"
- On sale day: "170 bags are sitting on that page with price tags that would make Alice cry."
- "We can't make all five. *(Warehouse space. Production timeline. Alice would kill me.)*"
- Converts to believer: "Alice just walked back into my office carrying a tape measure. She's measuring warehouse space for this weekend's orders."

**Alice's narrative function:**
1. **Stakes raiser** — her worry = this sale is REAL and financially risky
2. **Validation** — when ALICE is excited, readers know it's big
3. **Relatability** — represents the "logical friend" every entrepreneur has
4. **Comic tension** — her stress reactions are physically funny (breathing exercises, measuring tape)

### Tasha — Social Media & Fulfillment (The Hype Woman)
**Role:** Handles social media and monitors live sales. The most vocal, energetic team member. Loses her voice from yelling during sale days.

| Trait | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Focus** | Social media, live sales monitoring |
| **Personality** | High-energy, vocal, competitive, passionate |
| **Physical comedy** | Yells cities from across the office, loses voice, sits on floor with shoes off |
| **Defining moment** | Walks into Michelle's office "holding her phone like a weapon" |

**Greatest hits:**
- "Because every 90 seconds, Tasha yells from across the office: 'DETROIT. $9.95 THUNDARA. THAT'S THE THIRD ONE.'"
- After a sale day: "Tasha is sitting on the floor. Shoes OFF. Back against the wall. She's been yelling cities and bag names since 10 AM and her voice is completely gone."
- "George called me 10 minutes ago. I answered and all he heard was Tasha screaming 'BROOKLYN. $9.95.' in the background."
- On the No-Social-Media email: "Here's what Tasha CAN'T post publicly, but I CAN tell you..."
- "Tasha just texted me: 'Fine. But I'm getting one for my mom.'"

**Tasha's narrative function:**
1. **Energy barometer** — her volume = how well the sale is going
2. **Live sale narrator** — she calls the orders, creating real-time excitement
3. **Social media gatekeeper** — creates "insider" feel (can't post this on IG, only here)
4. **Physical comedy** — shoes off, voice gone, sitting on floor surrounded by pizza boxes

**Role:** Afroyla's Head Designer. Sketches new collections, obsesses over survey feedback, and panics beautifully during sale events.

| Trait | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Title** | Head Designer |
| **Personality** | Passionate, creative, intense, confrontational with George |
| **Physical comedy** | Bursts into Michelle's office, taps her watch, leaves shoes under desk |
| **Relationship with George** | Cannot tolerate him — screams in the hallway after his comments |

**Greatest hits:**
- "Keisha BURSTS into my office. 'MICHELLE! The survey feedback is INSANE. I'm obsessed with what we're creating.'"
- On BLS test: "Y'ALL ARE CHEATING" / "WAIT THERE WERE ONLY 3?!" / "Okay so I didn't get a $9.95 bag but I got 4 bags for under $250. I'm actually the biggest winner here."
- "Keisha just stared at him. Long pause. Blinked twice." (at George's design advice)
- "Everyone's gone home. Keisha's shoes are still under her desk. (She does this EVERY time.)"
- "Keisha is in the corner with her feet on a chair, eating breadsticks and staring at the ceiling like she just survived something."
- Post-George SEO comment: "Keisha walked out of the room. I heard her SCREAMING in the hallway."

**Keisha's narrative function:**
1. **Creative authority** — her excitement = the designs are good
2. **Product bridge** — connects stories to actual bag designs/collections
3. **George foil** — their interactions are pure comedy
4. **Community co-creation** — she and Michelle sketch together, surveys validate her designs

### Marcus — Warehouse Manager (The Quiet Soldier)
**Role:** Warehouse manager. A man of few words and precise logistics. The opposite of Tasha in every way.

| Trait | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Title** | Warehouse Manager |
| **Personality** | Quiet, reliable, factual, almost never smiles |
| **Communication** | Short sentences, texts from the warehouse, clipboard always in hand |
| **Physical comedy** | "Almost smiled" is treated as a major event |

**Greatest hits:**
- Before BLS: Texts from warehouse: "We ready."
- On clearance: "They're selling slowly," Marcus says carefully.
- "Marcus has packed so many wallet orders he's stopped talking. He just points at things now. I think we broke him."
- "Marcus almost smiled when we finished restacking. ALMOST. (He doesn't smile often. But when he does? You know everything's perfect.) 💜"
- "Marcus. Our warehouse manager." → "I put down my coffee. Marcus doesn't call unless it's serious."
- Sale day: "Marcus is eating with one hand. Taping boxes with the other. He hasn't stopped moving since 10:03 AM."

**Marcus's narrative function:**
1. **Scale indicator** — if Marcus is busy, orders are REAL
2. **Calm amid chaos** — contrast to everyone else's emotions
3. **Authenticity marker** — operations details (boxes, tape, clipboard) ground the story
4. **Rare emotion payoff** — when Marcus reacts, it LANDS

## 🔄 RUNNING GAGS & TRADITIONS

### George Gags
1. **"George. [Go/Stop/Why]."** — Michelle's deadpan shut-down format
2. **George "helping"** — shows up uninvited, makes suggestions about things he doesn't understand
3. **Bald hair jokes** — "I would look GREAT with natural hair" (he's bald)
4. **SEO = "Super Excellent Offer"** — peak George
5. **"(George if you're reading this, STOP.)"** — 4th-wall break
6. **"Don't tell him"** — when George is actually right

### Nia's Facts
- Every morning in February (BHM), Nia ambushes Michelle with a Black history fact
- Always starts before coffee: "My daughter stays teaching me things before I've had my coffee"
- Format: date → historical event → Nia's one-line commentary that levels Michelle
- Also appears in P.S.: "P.S. Nia told me this morning that on THIS day..."

### Sale Day Chaos
- Alice doing breathing exercises
- Tasha losing her voice
- Marcus going non-verbal
- Keisha staring at ceiling
- Multiple pizza boxes in the office
- George showing up uninvited

### Coffee As Motif
- Michelle always has coffee
- George brings coffee when "reporting for duty"
- "I put down my coffee" = something serious is happening
- "before I've had my coffee" = Nia ambush timing

## ✍️ HOW TO USE THIS IN NEW EMAILS

1. **Pick 1-2 cast members per email** — don't crowd the scene
2. **George = P.S./P.P.S. only** (unless sale day scene)
3. **Nia = opening OR P.S.** (never force; only when there's a real historical/wisdom connection)
4. **Alice = stakes** (bring her in when money/risk is involved)
5. **Tasha = live action** (sale day real-time narration)
6. **Keisha = product/creative** (new designs, survey results, collection reveals)
7. **Marcus = grounding** (warehouse reality, shipping, "the work is real")
8. **Always use specific details:** Time stamps, physical descriptions, dialogue format
9. **Maintain character voice:** George is clueless-sweet, Alice is cautious-converts, Tasha is loud, Marcus is quiet, Keisha is intense, Nia is wise-beyond-years
