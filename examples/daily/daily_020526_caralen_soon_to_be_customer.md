# DAILY | 020526 | Caralen — Soon-to-be-Customer

**Subject:** Soon-to-be-customer.
**Preview:** A category I didn't know I had until yesterday.

---

*"Character is key because that's who you are when nobody's watching you."*

That's the closing line of an email a Deaconess named Caralen sent me yesterday.

I read it. Closed my laptop. Went to make coffee.

Came back and read the whole email again.

Caralen owns one of our bags. She loves it. She wants another. She can't afford it right now.

She didn't write to ask for a discount. She wrote to tell me where she is.

Here's what she said. Word for word:

> *"I truly love your product! But even with the products on sale, I still can't afford them. My mom had been battling lung cancer and I was the only one taking her back and forth to treatments and all other appointments. And I'm battling anxiety and depression, but I was there for my mom. I only receive Social Security Disability and I fell short in some areas and now trying to catch up. And always helping out at Church for no pay because what I do I do for God, not for money or a pat on the back... I would really love another bag, I have 1 and I love it, I was working then. This is who I am. Deaconess Caralen Temoney ❤️"*

I read that twice. Then I sat with it.

And I realized she'd just introduced herself to a category I didn't know I had a name for.

Soon-to-be-customer.

Not "lapsed." Not "one-time." Not "lost sale." None of the words I'd been using in my head.

Soon-to-be.

She's not gone. She's just not ready. There's a difference.

Caralen has a list. Her mom comes first. Her own health second. Her church third. Then her.

The bag is on the list. It just isn't first. Yet.

Some women buy in May. Some buy in November. Some buy when their mom finishes chemo. All three are real. All three count.

So I'm going to put her email in the folder where I keep the ones I want to remember. I'll keep packing for the women whose moment is right now. And when Caralen's moment comes (it will), I'll be here.

We named one of our bags **Barakaya.** It means *Just Blessed.* Two words. The same two words a Deaconess might write at the bottom of a card.

That's the bag for Caralen. Not today. The day she decides today is the day.

The bag is patient. It can wait for her.
