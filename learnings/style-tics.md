### Emotion-label phrases (tidy beats / labeled emotions)

| Phrase | Why retired |
|--------|-------------|
| `She has a point.` / `That landed.` / `It hit me.` | Labels emotion instead of showing it. Tidy 3-line beats violate Show-Don't-Tell. Replace with action: "I sit there." / "I open the laptop. Click on six. Look at it longer than I meant to." |
| `It hit me before I could push it away` | Same problem — narrating thought arrival instead of letting it arrive |

### Meta-narration phrases (writer-on-stage)

| Phrase | Why retired |
|--------|-------------|
| `That was the [email/whole thing/point].` | Labels what just happened. Reader felt it. |
| `He's the reason this email has a [forward button/share button/...].` | Construction-narration. Replaced May 07 v10 with vaguer "He's the reason I wrote this." |
| `the answer to a problem I'd been wrestling with` | Telling reader they just witnessed an arc. |
| `That, weirdly, was the email.` | Same pattern. |

### Catchphrase cues (writers-room signals)

| Phrase | Why retired |
|--------|-------------|
| `Don't start.` (as Michelle's reply) | Sitcom catchphrase that signals "we are in a scripted comedy" — not Michelle's voice |

### Corporate / bureaucratic openers

| Phrase pattern | Why retired |
|----------------|-------------|
| `regarding our [X]` / `regarding the [Y]` | Dead corporate-memo word; "regarding" died in 2008 |
| `In yesterday's [survey/email/announcement], regarding...` | Same — bureaucratic register |
| `our selection of [X] [Y]` | Press-release voice |

### Tepid closes (permission, not invitation)

| Phrase pattern | Why retired |
|----------------|-------------|
| `Forward away.` / `[verb] away.` | Permission grant, not action ask |
| `Click around if you want.` / `Take a look.` | Soft, no urgency, no destination |

### Floating metaphor pairs (one half cut, other floating)

| Phrase | Reason |
|--------|--------|
| `the main door is still [X]` (without "side door" pair present) | Floating creative-language, reader doesn't have anchoring metaphor |
| `the side door / main door` pair | Don't reintroduce — proven confusing for non-native readers |

## 🧪 PRE-DRAFT WORKFLOW

Before writing opening 3 lines:

1. **Scan RETIRED list** — does my draft opener match any retired structure? If yes → change.
2. **Scan PROBATION list** — does my draft use a 2-strike pattern? If yes, prefer a fresh alternative from STILL FRESH.
3. **Scan last 5 examples** in `examples/daily/` — does my opener structure echo any of them? If yes → change.

Same workflow for closing imperatives, emotion-label beats, meta-narration phrases.

---

## 📝 UPDATE PROTOCOL

After each `/finalize-email`:

1. Scan the shipped email for any phrase from the **PROBATION** list. If found → move to **RETIRED**.
2. Scan for any phrase used 2+ times across the last 30 days of `examples/daily/` that's not yet on the registry → add to **PROBATION**.
3. Scan for any new phrase that lands well + is reusable → add to **STILL FRESH**.

**Registry is living.** Update it like a brand glossary, not a one-time document.
