# Afroyla Writing Patterns — Extracted from 170 Klaviyo Campaigns

> **Key insight:** Michelle's voice is the brand. Every email reads like a text from your smartest, funniest friend who happens to sell bags.

---

## 1. Voice & Tone

### Michelle's Persona
- **Warm but not soft** — She doesn't sugarcoat; she speaks directly
- **Conversational** — Reads like a phone call, not a newsletter
- **Emotional but grounded** — Deep feelings expressed in simple language
- **Self-deprecating humor** — George jokes, "apparently I hate my accountant"
- **Empowering without preaching** — Shows, doesn't tell

### Recurring Characters
| Character | Role | Frequency |
|-----------|------|-----------|
| **Michelle** | Narrator/CEO, always 1st person | Every email |
| **George** | Husband, comic relief P.P.S. | Very frequent |
| **Nia** | Daughter, daily facts/wisdom | Frequent in DAILY |
| **Alice/Keisha** | Team members | Occasional |
| **Tamara/Customer names** | Real or fictional story subjects | Story emails |

### Tone Spectrum by Email Type
- **STORY/DAILY:** Intimate, reflective, emotional → CTA feels earned
- **TEASER:** Playful, conspiratorial, building anticipation → "I shouldn't be telling you this"
- **SALE:** Energetic, urgent, chaotic-fun → "Girl, I WON the Games"

## 2. Structural Patterns

### The "Michelle Story Arc" (STORY/DAILY emails)
```
1. SCENE SETTER — Specific moment (time, place, sensory detail)
   "My phone rang at 9:47 PM last night."
   "I'm scrolling Instagram this morning. Coffee in one hand."

2. THE HOOK — Something unexpected happens
   "A post tagged @afroyla.handbags. From a man's account."
   "She just... didn't pick up the flat iron."

3. THE STORY — Build emotional momentum
   Short paragraphs. One-line sentences. 
   Dialogue in quotes. Parenthetical asides.

4. THE PIVOT — Connect story to bigger meaning
   "That's why I built these bags."
   "That's what her brother gave her. Not a bag. A witness."

5. SOFT CTA — Feels like natural conclusion
   "👑 She showed up anyway. So can you. → The Heritage Tote"
   "Shop the Collection" (centered, linked)

6. SIGN-OFF — "Love, Michelle"

7. P.S. — Nia fact (educational) or George joke (lightness)
```

### The "Urgency Machine" (SALE emails)
```
1. STATUS UPDATE — "If you could see my inbox/dashboard right now..."
2. SOCIAL PROOF — Real-time customer actions
3. SCARCITY — Specific numbers ("15 orders left", "First 50 gone")
4. DEAL RECAP — Clear pricing
5. DIRECT CTA — Button or bold link
6. DEADLINE — "Tonight at midnight" / "Ends in 3 hours"
```

### The "Buildup Machine" (TEASER emails)
```
1. NARRATIVE FRAME — Story format, not announcement
2. REVEAL ELEMENT — Partial info, curiosity gaps
3. COMMUNITY INCLUSION — "You're on the planning committee"
4. COUNTDOWN — Days/hours until event
5. SMS CROSS-SELL — "Text [KEYWORD] to 1-866-580-4283"
6. ANTICIPATION LOOP — "Tomorrow I'm revealing..."
```

## 3. Writing Techniques

### Sentence Construction
- **One-line paragraphs** dominate — almost never more than 2 sentences per `<p>`
- **Short sentences build rhythm:** "She paused." / "I sat there." / "That's the smile."
- **Em dash for emotional emphasis:** "Not a bag — a WITNESS."
- **ALL CAPS for emphasis** (sparingly): "That's the LOOK." / "THAT. SMILE."
- **Bold for key emotional beats:** **"She showed up as herself."**
- **Italics for inner voice/quotes:** *"it's too soon. They don't know me well enough."*

### Paragraph Rhythm
```
Long setup sentence with context and detail.

Short punch.

Another short punch.

Slightly longer reflection that adds meaning.

One-word emotional landing.
```

### The "Section Break" Pattern
- Uses `---` (rendered as horizontal rule) to break story sections
- Creates visual breathing room
- Signals a tonal shift (story → meaning → CTA)

### Parenthetical Voice
- **(This is going to sound dramatic, but stay with me.)**
- **(When Tamara says "I have to tell you about today," you sit down. You pour something. You listen.)**
- **(Earned it. Let that sit for a second.)**
- Creates intimate, conspiratorial feeling

## 4. Emotional Frameworks

### Core Themes (recurring across campaigns)
| Theme | How it appears | Example |
|-------|---------------|---------|
| **Being SEEN** | The bag as mirror, recognition | "Somebody made this... for ME" |
| **Showing up as yourself** | Natural hair, authenticity | "The room adjusted" |
| **Queen identity** | Crown, royalty language | "Rise and Slay", "Queen" |
| **Community/Sisterhood** | "Sis", "your cousins on this list" | "Girl, I WON the Games" |
| **Overcoming doubt** | Internal monologue about worthiness | "I haven't earned it yet" |
| **Black joy/pride** | Cultural celebration without apology | Kwanzaa, BHM, Tuskegee |
| **Family closeness** | George, Nia, personal moments | Braiding hair, morning routines |

### Emotional Altitude by Email Type
- **DAILY/STORY:** Deep → Peaks at pivot moment → Soft landing
- **TEASER:** Rising anticipation → Peaks at reveal/countdown
- **SALE:** High energy throughout → Peaks at scarcity + CTA

## 5. CTA Patterns

### Soft CTAs (Story/Daily)
- Centered text link: `👑 She showed up anyway. So can you. → The Heritage Tote`
- Simple text: `Shop the Collection` (hyperlinked)
- Embedded in narrative: "That artwork on the bag she's holding? [link]"

### Hard CTAs (Sale)
- Bold urgency: `[LIVE NOW] Legacy Collection - First 50 get best deal`
- Button style: Dark `Shop Now` buttons
- Multiple CTA insertions throughout long sale emails

### Cross-channel CTAs
- SMS signup: `Text "BLACK" to 1-866-580-4283`
- Always positioned as "cheat code" or "unfair advantage"

## 6. Sale Campaign Patterns

### Multi-Day Sale Structure
```
Day -7 to -1: TEASER emails (1-2/day)
Day 1: Opening email (Morning) → Update (Afternoon) → Last Drop (Evening)
Day 2-3: Same daily cadence
Final Day: 3-4 emails with escalating urgency
```

### Segmentation Strategy
- **Buy Herself** vs **Buy Gift** segments (different copy, same offer)
- Gift-giver copy emphasizes: "the auntie with good gifts"
- Self-purchase copy emphasizes: "you deserve this"

### Pricing Psychology
- **Anchor high, reveal low:** "$9.95 bags. Yes, really."
- **Stack value:** "You spend $80, walk away with $200 worth"
- **Hidden deals:** "Hidden in the collection" — treasure hunt mechanic
- **Tiered urgency:** First tier sells out → next tier announcement

## 7. P.S. Patterns

### Two Recurring P.S. Formats

**Nia's Daily Fact (educational):**
> **P.S.** Nia told me this morning that on THIS day — February 19th, 1942 — the Tuskegee Airmen were activated. Black men told they weren't smart enough to fly combat planes. She looked at me and said, "They showed up anyway, Mom."

**George Joke (comic relief):**
> **P.P.S.** George heard Tamara's story and said, "I would look GREAT with natural hair." George is bald. 💀

### P.S. Function
- Lightens emotional weight after heavy story
- Reinforces family/personal brand
- Adds "reward" for reading to the end
- Sometimes contains secondary CTA

## 8. Key Copywriting Rules (inferred)

1. **Never announce, always narrate** — Even sale emails start with a story
2. **One emotion per email** — Don't try to make them laugh AND cry AND buy
3. **The CTA must match the emotional altitude** — Don't hard-sell after making someone cry
4. **Short paragraphs are law** — 1-2 sentences max per paragraph
5. **Names make it real** — Tamara, not "a friend"; 9:47 PM, not "last night"
6. **The bag is never just a bag** — It's a mirror, a witness, recognition, a crown
7. **Always close the loop** — If you open with a question, answer it
8. **P.S. is mandatory** — It's where the personality lives
9. **Personalization is subtle** — {{ first_name }} greeting, but content isn't hyper-personalized
10. **Frequency is high but format varies** — 1-2 emails/day but never feels repetitive because type rotates
