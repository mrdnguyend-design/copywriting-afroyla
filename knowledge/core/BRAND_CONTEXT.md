### Brand Story
- Founded by Michelle, who moved with her family to Vietnam
- Previously lived in Bali, Ghana, South Africa, Cambodia, Thailand
- Has a degree in retail with concentration in fashion merchandising
- Started as "Melanista" in 2024 — made-to-order handbags
- Rebranded to "Afroyla" in 2025 — shifted to ready-to-ship US-based inventory
- Mission: luxury-inspired handbags accessible and fast for Black women

### Product Lines
- **Leather Handbags** — Everyday staples with standout style
- **Leather Tote Bags** — Roomy, reliable, refined
- **Leather Round Bags** — Modern, bold, made to be noticed
- **Mini Satchels** — Small but mighty (premium vegan leather)
- **Leather Wallets** — Clean lines, deep pride
- **[Coming Soon] Bag Charms** — Personalization accessories

### Brand Voice
- "Sister-to-sister" connection
- CEO character: Michelle — proud, unapologetic, bold
- Philosophy: "Entertain first, Empower second, Sell third"
- Afroyla is "the prize" — never desperate for sales
- Polarization is essential — content should deeply resonate with ideal customers while potentially alienating others

## 3. TARGET AUDIENCE

- **Primary:** Black women, 35+
- **Profile:** Successful, proud of their roots, tired of mainstream luxury that ignores them
- **Psychographic:** Values cultural heritage, self-investment, unapologetic boldness
- **Villains:** Euro-centric luxury (brands that don't respect them), fast fashion (cheap bags that peel), erasure (society trying to dim their light)

## 4. MARKETING STRATEGIST CONTEXT

**Duc** works as marketing strategist for Afroyla.
- Bilingual communication (Vietnamese/English)
- Deep understanding of direct response marketing psychology
- Primary focus: sophisticated email marketing using "Infotainment Jackpot" methodology

### Methodology
- **Ben Settle** + **Daniel Throssell** frameworks
- Character-driven storytelling over traditional promotional copy
- Techniques: Scene Stacking, Seinfeld Method, Simplicio, Polarization
- "Hot Dog vs Broccoli" principle
- Success measured by: engagement, cultural authenticity, premium positioning

### Campaign Development Approach
1. Initial brainstorming
2. Expert persona feedback (Ben Settle / Daniel Throssell critiques)
3. Rewrites incorporating suggestions
4. HTML formatting with specific styling
5. Comprehensive documentation (subject lines, strategic frameworks)

### Key Principles
- **Character-first, not product-first** — handbags are byproducts of Michelle's worldview
- **Confidence without apology** — Afroyla is "the prize"
- **Authentic cultural celebration** — community-owned business, not performative
- **Entertainment + clear CTAs** — sale details must appear early before entertainment
- **Polarization** — apologetic or overly inclusive language dilutes brand power
- **Real stories** — customer experiences, family interactions, behind-the-scenes moments

### Audience Segmentation
- **MASS** — Full subscriber list
- **HIGH-ENGAGED** — Active openers/clickers
- **MLK-Registered** / **BLS-Registered** — Campaign-specific segments
- Different messaging frequencies and channels per segment

### Technical Specs
- HTML emails with custom span tags and strategic styling
- SMS via toll-free 1-866-580-4283 with keyword opt-ins
- Landing pages: Pan-African color schemes, luxury typography (Playfair Display, Cormorant Garamond, Montserrat)
- Dual-state systems for pre-launch vs live campaign messaging

### Cultural Contexts
- **Black History Month** (February)
- **MLK Weekend** (January)
- **Juneteenth** (June 19)
- **Mother's Day**, Valentine's Day, Christmas
- Community impact: donations to **Black Girls Code**, **Loveland Foundation**
- Balance commercial objectives with genuine community impact
- Use empowerment/investment framing, NOT charity-focused language
