# TEASER | 100426 | Generous or Polite

**Subject:** Sometimes I think I'm generous. I'm not sure anymore.
**Preview:** Somebody replied to my email Tuesday. I wasn't ready.

---

{{ person.first_name|default:'love' }},

Sometimes I think I'm generous. Then somebody sends me an email and I realize I'm just... polite.

There's a difference. I found out on Tuesday.

I sent you an email about Jovita. The mother who bought her daughter an Afroyla bag after she left an abusive situation. I ended with a question: *who are you buying for this Mother's Day?*

Most of you answered. Your mama. Your sister. Yourself. *(Yes, yourself counts.)*

But one reply didn't answer my question. She told me about her best friend instead.

---

*"I was texting my bff from like 23 years ago. I haven't seen her in 11 years or so and that was only because I traveled back to my home state often back then. Settling down, buying a house, and creating a life here has led to us calling and texting less but still happy when we do. I wanted to see her again so much that I was texting her this morning and asked if she would come if I flew her out for my birthday. At 35, she has never flown on an airplane in her life and with her disability it makes it hard for her to get around. I just stopped in the middle of taking laundry downstairs, sat down, and bought her a first class ticket with curb to curb wheelchair assistance and lounge access, to spend the summer with me because if this might be her only flight in her life, she deserves a first class window seat."*

---

I got to the end and scrolled back up. Not because I missed something. Because I wasn't done feeling it.

She stopped in the middle of carrying laundry downstairs. Sat down on the stairs. And bought her best friend a first class ticket. Not coach. Not basic economy. First class. Window seat. Wheelchair assistance. Lounge access. The whole thing.

Not because she had money to throw around. Not because it was easy. Because after 23 years and 11 years apart and a disability that makes it hard for her to get around... she wanted her friend next to her badly enough that the rest didn't matter.

That's not impulse. That's love that's been sitting there for 11 years, waiting for the moment she could do something about it.

And here's the part that got me.

She didn't plan it. She didn't sleep on it. She didn't open three tabs and compare flights for two weeks. She was carrying laundry. She sat down. She bought the ticket.

When you care about someone that much, you don't need to think about it. The decision already made itself a long time ago. You were just waiting for the moment to act on it.

I think about that a lot. How many times I've told someone "I miss you" and then... done nothing. How many times I've said "we should get together" and meant it, really meant it, but then Tuesday turned into next month and next month turned into "it's been a year already?"

This woman didn't do that. She sat down on the stairs with a pile of dirty clothes and she did the thing.

I don't know if I'll ever be the woman who stops on the stairs and just does the thing. But I know what it looks like now. And that's a start.
