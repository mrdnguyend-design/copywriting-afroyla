# 🎯 BEN & DANIEL CRITIQUE — Apology Email (saleday_210326_apology_oversold.md)

---

## 🎯 BEN'S REVIEW
================

**Hook Power:** ⭐⭐⭐ / 5
"I need to be straight with you." — Decent. Direct. But it's a GENERIC directness. Any CEO could write this. Michelle's version would be more specific: a TIME, an ACTION, a FEELING. "I've been sitting at this desk since 4AM" > "I need to be straight with you." The reader should feel like they interrupted Michelle mid-crisis.

**Personality:** ⭐⭐ / 5
This reads like a GOOD apology email from A BRAND. Not like Michelle. The V1 says things like "That is not OK, and I am genuinely sorry" which is correct but corporate. Michelle would say something like "I sat in my car in the parking lot for 10 minutes before coming inside because I didn't want to write this email." The FEELING should be specific, not stated. Rule #3 from learnings: "Michelle must be IN the story, not just narrating from outside."

**Status Positioning:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ / 5
The 80% OFF offer is legitimately generous. The "not the sale page, the ENTIRE store" distinction works. But the framing "So here's what I have for you" feels like a customer service script. Michelle would make it feel like she FOUGHT for this: "Alice looked at me like I lost my mind when I typed 80%."

**Sales Psychology:** ⭐⭐⭐ / 5
The discount code at the end is fine but the email lacks PROOF. User wants screenshot of the oversold products — this is GENIUS. Showing the negative inventory numbers = radical transparency = trust. No brand does this. Michelle showing her dashboard with -661 stock = "I'm not hiding anything from you."

**Line-by-line improvements:**
- Line: "I need to be straight with you."
  → Better: Drop into a scene. Michelle sitting at her desk, 4AM, staring at the screen.
  → Why: Style Rule #3 — Michelle must be IN the story.

- Line: "That is not OK, and I am genuinely sorry."
  → Better: Show the sorry through ACTION, not STATEMENT. "I have rewritten this email four times."
  → Why: Show don't tell. A stated apology is corporate. A shown apology is Michelle.

- Line: "But I want to do more than just give you your money back. Because that doesn't fix the feeling."
  → Better: Good instinct, but "fix the feeling" is a LINE. Michelle would be messier: "A refund gives you your $9.95 back. It doesn't give you back the excitement you had when you hit 'place order.'"
  → Why: Specificity. Name the exact emotional loss.

- Line: "I am personally embarrassed that this happened"
  → Better: "This is one of the hardest emails I've written since I started Afroyla."
  → Why: User direction. This is the VULNERABILITY that makes it Michelle.

## 🎨 DANIEL'S REVIEW
====================

**Scene Construction:** ⭐⭐ / 5
There IS no scene. The V1 is an explanation + offer. That's a customer service template with Michelle's name on it. Where is it 4AM? Where is Michelle staring at the screen? Where is Alice texting her "how bad is it"? The SCENE is what separates this from every other apology email on the internet. Show Michelle IN the crisis, not EXPLAINING the crisis.

**Dialogue:** ⭐ / 5
Zero dialogue. Not one line. This is the email where Marcus says "...it's bad." This is where Alice stops counting mid-spreadsheet. There should be at least ONE moment where a character reacts to what happened.

**Show vs Tell:** ⭐⭐ / 5
Almost entirely TELLING.
- "I stayed up late sorting through it" → SHOW what she saw. Show the screen. Show the numbers going negative.
- "I am genuinely sorry" → SHOW the sorry. "I've rewritten this email four times. Three of them started with 'Dear Customer.' I deleted those."
- "I am personally embarrassed" → "This is the hardest email I've written since starting Afroyla." (User's exact direction.)

**Emotional Pacing:** ⭐⭐⭐ / 5
The structure is: bad news → explanation → refund → offer. Fine. But the EMOTIONAL arc should be: vulnerability (hardest email) → radical transparency (screenshot) → specific apology (naming what they lost) → making it right (80% OFF with Alice reaction) → warmth (I want you to love something). The V1 is functional. The V2 needs to be FELT.

**Line-by-line improvements:**
- Line: "Yesterday during March Madness, something went wrong on our end."
  → Better: Open with Michelle in the moment. "I've been staring at my screen since 4AM."
  → Why: Scene beats summary. Every time. Learnings Rule #4: "Inhabit scenes, don't summarize them."

- Line: "Your refund is being processed right now. You don't need to do anything."
  → Better: "Your refund is already processing. You'll see it back in your account within 48 hours. You don't need to do anything."
  → Why: User requirement (48h timeline) + specificity = trust.

- Line: (No screenshot reference)
  → Better: Embed the screenshot of Shopify dashboard showing negative inventory. "Here's what my screen looked like when I found out:"
  → Why: User requirement. Radical transparency. No brand shows this. Trust builder.
