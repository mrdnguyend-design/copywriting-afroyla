Here's the thing about Tamara — she just started at a new firm. Corporate law. The kind of office where everybody's suit looks the same and nobody's lunch smells like anything.

She'd been flat-ironing her hair every morning since the interview. Three weeks straight. Getting up an hour early. Pressing it down. Making it "neat."

But this morning — the flat iron was sitting on the counter, already plugged in — and she just... didn't pick it up.

She looked in the mirror. Her hair was OUT. Full. Big.

"I stood there for like ten minutes," she told me. "I had my hand on the flat iron. I was thinking — it's too soon. They don't know me well enough. I haven't earned it yet."

She walked out the door with her hair exactly how it grew out of her head.

She got to her desk. Put her bag down. Opened her laptop.

By lunch — three people had complimented her.

But that's not the part that made her call me at 9:47 PM.

By 3 PM — a partner stopped her in the hallway. Not about a case. Not about a brief.
