But how crazy it gets?

That part is up to you.

Because YOU’RE the one carrying these bags to brunch, to church, to work.
Not my spreadsheets. Not my warehouse.

So if anyone should design this sale?

It’s you.

So tell me, my love… what kind of chaos are we starting?

OPTION A: The “Lucky Refund” Madness

You check out like normal.
You pay. You get your bag. Life is good.

And then a few random queens get an email like:

“SURPRISE.
Your entire order has been refunded.
You still keep the bag.”

Husband: “HOW MUCH DID YOU SPEND?!”
You: “Actually it was free. Talk to Michelle.”

Petty?
Chaotic?
Yes. I kind of love it.


OPTION B: The “Secret Code in the Email” Hunt

Somewhere in my Black Friday emails…
I hide a secret discount code.

Not in the obvious places.
Not in a big red button.

Maybe in a P.S.
Maybe hidden in a typo.
Maybe in a fake “unsubscribe” line. 👀

You’re on the couch, zooming in on the email like:
“Wait… why is that word bold?”

First queens to crack the code?
Get the BIGGEST discount of the whole sale.


OPTION C: The “Mystery Gift” Trap (The Fun Kind)

At checkout, a little box pops up:

“Add a mystery gift to your order?”

You can’t see what it is.
You don’t know the value.

You just have to trust:
“Michelle is not about to send me a pencil and a sticker.”

Then on delivery day, you’re in your kitchen, opening that box like a 7-year-old at Christmas.
Screaming when you see what’s inside.
Texting your group chat:
“Y’ALL. I GOT THE MYSTERY THING.”


OPTION D: “Michelle, sit down. I have an even crazier idea.”

Maybe you want:

  • A prize for the highest cart total.
  • A “Queens Only Power Hour” with secret deals.
  • Or: “Tell me your story and I’ll pick 5 women and send their bags 100% free.”

If your brain is cooking something wild?

I want to hear it.

Here’s what I need from you:

Hit reply and tell me:

“I vote A / B / C / D and here’s why…”

You can mix them too:

“B + C, but make the puzzle easy, I’m tired, girl.”

I’m reading these myself.
And yes — I will literally use your answers when I lock in the sale.

This is not “marketing research.”
This is our sale.

Now… while we’re plotting the MOST INSANE DEALS in Afroyla history…

We quietly added a new promotion on every product in the store.

No announcement. No big graphic.
Just… live.

“Hey sis… am I cute enough for you?”

or “no ma’am, this is not Black Friday-level yet.” 😂
