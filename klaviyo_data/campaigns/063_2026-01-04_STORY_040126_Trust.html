"There is so much scamming going on in this world right now that I cannot believe or trust anything these days."

Oof.

And then?

DELIVERED TO PARCEL LOCKER.

Only one problem.

Rhonda doesn't HAVE a parcel locker. She lives in a house.

No package on the porch. No note on the door. No friendly UPS guy waving from the street.

Just… nothing.

She walked around her yard. (Maybe they left it by the side gate?)

Nope.

Asked the neighbors. (Maybe they took it inside for safekeeping?)

Nope.

Called USPS. (Maybe there's a reasonable explanation?)

NOPE.

So she emailed us.

And honey, I FELT that line about scamming. Because she's right.

In 2026? You can't trust NOTHING.

Fake tracking numbers. Porch pirates. Phishing emails that look so real your own mama would click them.

How do you know who's legit anymore?

We checked our side. Contacted the carrier. Did everything we could.

But here's the ugly truth about online business: once that package gets scanned "delivered," we're just as powerless as you are.

All we've got is email. And hope. And our word.

Rhonda didn't give up though.

She PRINTED OUT her tracking history. (Yes, printed. Like it's 1999.)

Took it to the post office IN PERSON.

They told her: "Give us a few days."

And waited.

Five days later...

She opens her front door.

And there it sits.

Her Afroyla bag.

No explanation. No apology note. Just… THERE.

Like it had been there all along, waiting for her to notice it. (The audacity of the postal service, I swear.)

Her next email to us?

"WOW — it is absolutely beautiful! It is more beautiful in person than in the pictures. I should have ordered the wallet too!"

Y'all.
