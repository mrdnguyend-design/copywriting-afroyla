{{ person.first_name|default:'love' }},

Keisha and I have been arguing for fifteen minutes.

9:04 this morning. Her sketchbook on my desk. My coffee going cold. One of the pages — not all of them, ONE — we can't agree on.

I don't love it.

She thinks I'm missing the point.

I told her I'd send this email and let the queens decide.

She told me to stop stalling and write it.

So here we are.

I know — yesterday was the last day of the sale. I haven't caught my breath yet. Keisha doesn't care.

---

Let me back up.

End of March, I showed you this:

[IMAGE: Afro Backpack prototype — handmade, afro silhouette stitched on leather, Keisha's workshop bench]

A prototype. Keisha made her. Completely different from anything on our site.

I asked you one question: "Do you want it?"

Almost 500 of you said YES.

I forwarded every single one of them to Keisha. She didn't bring the backpack up again for a while.

Then she burst into my office carrying a sketchbook thick enough to stop a door. I thought she'd brought me the finished sample. She hadn't.

She'd made six.

Six new designs. Six different women. Six different crowns.

I stared at the pages and didn't speak for a minute.

Then I said, "Keisha. Pick three. You can only cut three in time for Back-to-School."

She said, "No. THEY pick three."

---

So we're doing that.

👉 The survey is here: https://products.forms.app/afroyla-backpack

Three minutes. Rate each design 1 to 5. "Not my style" to "I love it!"

The three y'all rate highest go into production for Back-to-School. The other three stay in Keisha's sketchbook. Maybe forever.

And the one we're still arguing about?

I'm not telling you which one. I want to see if you agree with me or with Keisha.

If you side with her, she's going to be insufferable about it for the next six months. Worth it. I need the data.

Go.

Love,
Michelle

**P.S.** George walked past the sketchbook this morning. Before he'd even opened it: "Which one wins?" I closed the cover. "Not your call, George." He was offended. Keisha wasn't.

**P.P.S.** Keisha asked me to tell you: if you hate one, give it a 1. She won't be insulted. She'll be grateful. "One less I have to talk Michelle into."
