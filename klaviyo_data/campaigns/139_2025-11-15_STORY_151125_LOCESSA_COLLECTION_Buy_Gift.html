This morning… my sisters almost gave me a heart attack.

My family group chat went from zero to “WHO’S READY FOR A HAIR TRANSFORMATION???” in 3 seconds flat.

I’m pouring my coffee… still half-asleep… bonnet hanging on for dear life…

And suddenly —

“OKAY LADIES. I THINK I’M GETTING LOCS.”

Sent by my oldest sister like it was a national announcement.

Before I can even respond, the replies start flying:

Sister #2: “YESSS, I’ve admired locs my whole life.”
Sister #3: “They just feel… deeper. Like spiritual.”
Sister #4: “Girl I want something that makes me feel CONNECTED.”
Sister #5: “Plus I’m tired of chasing stressed stylists. Locs = PEACE.”
Sister #2 again: “Confidence finna go through the ROOF.”

I’m just staring at the phone like:

“Um… what is happening??”

Then they come for ME.

Sister #3: “Michelle… you’re suspiciously quiet.”
Sister #4: “We see you. Don’t run.”

Ma’am, I was not running. I was simply trying to finish my coffee before joining y’all’s spiritual awakening.

But as I kept reading… something shifted.

Because underneath all the chaos and GIFs and “loc goals” photos…

there was something real.

One sister said:

“My loose 4C hair is beautiful… but locs feel like I can finally breathe. Like I’m connected to everything around me.”

Another typed:

“Locs would make me feel more ME. More confident. More grounded.”

And suddenly… it clicked.

This wasn’t just hair talk.
This was identity talk.
Connection talk.
Confidence talk.

The kind of talk that means:

“She’s stepping into a new version of herself.”

And that’s when it hit me…

When a Black woman is entering a transformation era… you bring something that honors that transformation.
