Keisha texted me at 9:14 PM on a Tuesday.

"Can you hop on a call? Google Meet. Right now."

Right now.

You have to understand something about Keisha. Our Head Designer has a 5 PM hard stop that she enforces like a federal law. Phone face-down. Laptop shut. Shows on. I have been sent to voicemail at 5:03 PM by my own employee — the something she was busy with was Bridgerton Season 4 — and she has never once apologized for it.

So when Keisha texts right now at 9 PM on a Tuesday?

Something is either very wrong. Or very, VERY right.

I closed the bedroom door — Eli and Nia were staging what sounded like a full WWE pay-per-view in the living room — grabbed my laptop, and clicked the link.

Keisha’s face filled the screen. Sketchbook already open on her lap. Big grin.

"Michelle. I’ve been thinking."

(When Keisha says I’ve been thinking with THAT grin, I start sweating. It means she already made the decision. She’s just now informing me.)

"What if..." She flipped the sketchbook toward the camera. "...we made a bag with no artwork."

I stared at her.

"No... artwork?"

"No woman on the front. No crown. No dotwork. No affirmations." She reached off-camera and lifted something up to the lens — a swatch of leather that swallowed the entire screen. Deep maroon. The kind of color you see on a wine bottle in a dimly lit restaurant and immediately think I want that.

"Just this," she said quietly. "The leather. That’s it."

I didn’t say anything for a moment.

Because the artwork is our THING. That’s what makes people cry in their car after opening the box. The lady on the bag looks like me. You take that away — what are we even selling?

"Keisha. The artwork is literally why people buy from us—"

"Then why do they keep asking for something plain?"

...

She had me.

Because I’VE read those emails. The woman in Atlanta who bought three artwork bags and then wrote: "Michelle, do you have anything I can carry to the office?" The one who wanted to gift her sister but said: "She’d love the quality, but the artwork isn’t her style — do you have something simpler?"

I just hadn’t put them together. Until Keisha held a maroon swatch up to my screen at 9 PM and made me look at it.

"They’re not leaving us, Michelle." She leaned back and crossed her arms. "They want to buy MORE. They just need a bag for the other days of the week."

She’s right.

So we’re doing it. Plain leather. No artwork. Eight colors. And since I can’t produce all of them at once, I need to choose — which is exactly what I was explaining to Keisha when—

George walked in.

(Of course he did.)

"Babe. Who you talking to?"

"Keisha. We’re working."

He squinted at the screen. "At 9 o’clock?"

"George—"

Too late. He was already leaning over my shoulder, peering at the leather swatches on Keisha’s screen.

"Oh, new bags? What color?"

"We don’t know yet. There are EIGHT different colors and we need to figure out which ones to—"

"Black."

He said it before I finished the sentence.

"George."

He ignored her. "I’m just saying — technically, black absorbs all visible wavelengths of light." He pointed at me like he’d won something. "Eighth-grade science."

Keisha and I looked at each other through the screen.

"Baby. I love you. Go check on the kids."
