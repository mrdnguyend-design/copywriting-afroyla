## 1. Audit

- ✅ **Stunted Pacing:** NOT present. Appropriate pacing for a short apology email. Not rushing.
- ✅ **Missing the Point:** NOT present. The email correctly identifies this is about trust, not storytelling.
- ✅ **Lead Failure:** NOT present. "I need to tell you something about your order" = Relevant + Brief. Works.
- ✅ **Muddled Argument:** NOT present. Single thread: what happened → proof → refund → compensation → close.
- ✅ **Weak Insight:** N/A for apology email.
- ✅ **No Sales Desire:** N/A. This is NOT a sales email. The code is compensation, not a pitch. Correctly positioned.
- ⚠️ **Em Dash:** 1 em dash found. Line: "That's because the volume of orders yesterday was massive — and many customers were buying multiple products." **Within limit.** But review below for alternative.

### Minor Issues Found:

1. **"I need to tell you something about your order."** → Slightly ominous. Customer reads this and ALREADY feels dread. Consider softening or getting to the point faster — the subject line already signals what's coming.

2. **"And I am sorry."** → Standalone paragraph. Effective. BUT: "I am" is formal. Michelle says "I'm." The full "I am" reads like a legal statement. **Keep "I am" — in this context the formality signals weight. This is intentional.**

3. **"I know. The oversold numbers look massive."** → "I know." is doing double duty: acknowledging the reader's reaction AND transitioning from the image. Works. No change needed.

4. **"But I didn't want to stop there."** → "But" opening after the refund paragraph. Functional connector. However, "But I didn't want to stop there" is slightly performative. Simpler: "But a refund alone didn't feel like enough."

5. **"So I want to give you something that I hope shows that."** → "I hope shows that" is vague. Shows WHAT? The satisfaction mattering? Tighten: "So I want to give you something beyond the refund."

6. **"An exclusive code, just for customers affected by this:"** → "exclusive" is a marketing word. In an apology email, it reads slightly salesy. Simpler: "A code, just for you and the other customers affected by this:"

7. **"Thank you for your patience with us."** → "Patience" implies they've been WAITING for something. They've been waiting less than 24 hours. Better: "Thank you for understanding." Or simply: "Thank you."

8. **Subject line: "About your order — I owe you an explanation."** → Em dash in subject line. Fine stylistically, but "I owe you an explanation" is slightly dramatic for Michelle. Alternative: "About your order." (Period. Simple. Let the email explain.)
