# DAILY | 240226 | She Healed Without Permission

**Subject Line:** Nobody gave her permission to be a doctor.
**Preview Text:** My daughter told me this over breakfast.

---

{{ person.first_name|default:'love' }},

6:47 AM. Kitchen.

Nia's got her phone propped against the cereal box. Backpack on the floor. Toast half-eaten. She's reading something with that face she makes when she's about to ruin my whole morning with a fact.

I'm pouring coffee when she says — out of NOWHERE:

"Mom. Did you know there was a woman in 1864 who became a doctor when nobody thought she should?"

I looked at her over the mug.

"Her name was Rebecca," she said. "Rebecca Lee Crumpler."

I sat down.

"She grew up watching her aunt take care of sick people in the neighborhood. No degree. No office. Just... showing up. Every time somebody was hurting, her aunt was THERE."

The kitchen got quiet.

"So Rebecca became a nurse. For EIGHT YEARS, Mom. And then she applied to medical school."

Pause.

"She was the ONLY Black woman there."

I put my coffee down.

"They almost didn't let her finish," Nia said. Quieter now. "Her husband got sick. He died. And the school wasn't sure they wanted to re-enroll her."

She finally looked up from her phone.

"But people fought for her. And she graduated. March 1, 1864. First Black woman in America to earn an M.D."

"And you know what she did AFTER that, Mom?"

I shook my head.

"She moved to Richmond. To take care of people who had just been freed. People who had NOTHING. No doctor would see them. Nobody cared."

She paused.

"She didn't wait for anyone to give her permission to heal."

---

The toast got cold. The coffee got cold. I didn't care.

This child is in 5th grade and she's out here dismantling me before the school bus arrives.

"Nia," I said.

"Yeah?"

"Thank you."

She grabbed her backpack. Grinned. Headed for the door.

---

I sat at that kitchen table until I heard the bus pull away.

Rebecca Lee Crumpler didn't wait for the world to be ready for her. She didn't wait for the room to make space. She didn't wait for permission to show up.

She just SHOWED UP.

And yesterday, a woman named Crystal sent us a review that stopped me right there.

She said: *"This handbag and these wallets speak before you ever say a word."*

Read that again.

**They speak before you ever say a word.**

That's not a bag review, {{ person.first_name|default:'love' }}.

That's what Rebecca Lee Crumpler did. She walked into rooms and her PRESENCE spoke. Before she opened her mouth. Before she showed her degree. Before anyone gave her permission.

You stop waiting for the room to adjust.

The room adjusts to YOU.

The woman who's still waiting for the right moment, the right sale, the right everything before she lets herself be seen?

She's still waiting.

Rebecca wasn't her.
