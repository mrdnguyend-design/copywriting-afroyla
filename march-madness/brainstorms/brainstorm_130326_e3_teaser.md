## IDEAS

### IDEA 1: "Anne Demanded the Front Row" — History × Luxury Access ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Source:** March 13 Black History — Anne Wiggins Brown, first Black woman at Juilliard. When Porgy & Bess came to Washington D.C., she refused to perform unless the audience was integrated. They integrated the National Theater for the first time in its history. For ONE night. Because Anne said so.

**Hot Dog:** 1935. Anne Wiggins Brown walks into Juilliard. First Black woman they'd ever admitted. They didn't change the rules for her. They didn't make it easy. She just showed up and was undeniable.

Then Porgy & Bess went to D.C. The National Theater said: "Sure, come perform. But the audience stays segregated." Anne said no. "Then you don't get the show."

They integrated the theater. For the first time. For one night. Because she refused to perform for a room that didn't include her people.

**Bridge:** Some rooms don't open until someone refuses to accept the closed door. This sale? Same energy. Luxury leather at prices that say "this room is for everyone now." Not "affordable luxury" or "budget-friendly." Just: the door is open. Walk through it.

**Broccoli:** March Madness. March 20-22. From $9.95. Text AFR995E.

**Subject Lines:**

- "She refused to sing."
- "1935. They opened the theater for one night."
- "The door is open. Walk through it."

**Why it works:** Women's History Month tie-in. Powerful without being heavy. "Luxury for everyone" message delivered through history, not through marketing. Different from E1/E2 completely.

**Bridge:** This is what happens when you announce prices that don't make sense. People forward your email to their sister. Set alarms for a sale that's 8 days away.

**Broccoli:** March Madness. March 20-22. From $9.95. Text AFR995E.

**Subject Lines:**

**Why it works:** Social proof through RAW voices (Rule #8: paste raw words). Shows momentum. Funny and light. No heavy lifting.

### IDEA 3: "She Hadn't Bought Herself Anything in Years" — Self-Care ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Source:** Reddit — "I had not bought myself any new stuff for years. I treat that as a form of self-care."

**Hot Dog:** Michelle sits at the kitchen table. Reading replies. Most are excited, some are skeptical, one catches her. A woman who doesn't say which bag she wants. She says: "I haven't bought myself something nice in three years. Not because I can't. Because I kept putting everyone else first."

Michelle knows this woman. Not personally. But she knows her. She's every woman who buys school shoes before her own shoes, who buys birthday gifts for everybody but never treats herself. Who says "maybe next time" so often it stopped being a real sentence.

**Bridge:** March Madness wasn't designed for women who buy everything. It was designed for the woman who says "maybe next time" every time. Next time is March 20th.

**Broccoli:** $9.95 wallets. $14.95 handbags. Three days. Text AFR995E.

**Subject Lines:**

- "She hadn't bought herself anything in three years."
- "Not because she can't. Because she kept putting everyone else first."
- "Next time is March 20th."

**Why it works:** Emotional, target the woman who needs PERMISSION to spend on herself. Contrasts with E1 (business decision) and E2 (comedy). But may be too serious for teaser #3.
