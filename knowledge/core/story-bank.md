### S-020: "Freedom Bags" — 1940s Black Women Carried Clothes in Their Purses
- **Raw Material:** Cultural history: In the 1940s, Black women domestic workers used larger purses called "freedom bags" to carry a change of clothes — they had to arrive at employers' homes in uniform but needed their own clothes for the journey. Purses = portable privacy. Rosa Parks "dropped" her purse in the white section of a bus in 1943 (12 years before her famous arrest) to claim an adjacent seat. Purses have served "ornamental, utilitarian, psychological, linguistic, aesthetic, symbolic and economic functions" in American culture (Kathleen Casey, *The Things She Carried*, Oxford 2025). A purse is "an extension of the body, allowing women to carry a miniature version of the home."
- **Potential Theme:** Cultural Heritage + Product Story
- **Emotional Potential:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Source:** Ms. Magazine / Kathleen Casey, "The Things She Carried" (book excerpt, Aug 2025)
