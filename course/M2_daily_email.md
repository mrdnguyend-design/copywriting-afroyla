## 2.1 The Michelle Story Arc (6-Part Structure)

Extracted từ 70+ daily/story emails và pattern analysis of 170 Klaviyo campaigns:

### Structure

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ 1. SCENE SETTER — Specific moment                       │
│    "My phone rang at 9:47 PM last night."               │
│    "I'm scrolling Instagram. Coffee in one hand."       │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ 2. THE HOOK — Something unexpected                      │
│    "A post tagged @afroyla. From a man's account."      │
│    "She just... didn't pick up the flat iron."          │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ 3. THE NARRATIVE — Build emotional momentum             │
│    Short paragraphs. One-line sentences.                │
│    Dialogue in quotes. Parenthetical asides.            │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ 4. THE PIVOT — Story → bigger meaning                   │
│    "That's why I built these bags."                     │
│    "Not a bag. A witness."                              │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ 5. SOFT CTA — Natural conclusion                        │
│    "👑 She showed up anyway. → The Heritage Tote"       │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ 6. P.S./P.P.S. — Nia fact + George joke                │
│    P.S. = Tuskegee Airmen fact                          │
│    P.P.S. = "George is bald. 💀"                       │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Paragraph Rhythm

```
Long setup sentence with context and detail.

Short punch.

Another short punch.

Slightly longer reflection that adds meaning.

One-word emotional landing.
```

> **Rule:** 1-2 sentences per paragraph MAX. One-line paragraphs dominate.

### Signal Phrases by Section

| Section | Signal | Example |
|---------|--------|---------|
| Scene opener | Time + place + sensory | "6:52 AM. Kitchen. Still dark outside." |
| Hook | Disruption | "Then she said something that stopped me." |
| Escalation | Short declaratives | "She paused. I sat there. That's the smile." |
| Pivot | Bridge statement | "That's what Afroyla is. Not a bag — a mirror." |
| CTA | Emotional + link | "👑 Shop the Collection" |
| Sign-off | Always same | "Love, Michelle" |

## 2.2 The 21 Style Rules

> Đọc section này TRƯỚC khi viết bất kỳ email nào. Rút từ 12+ post-mortems và user feedback.

### Content Rules

| # | Rule | Why |
|---|------|-----|
| 1 | **ONE story per email** | Don't cram Karen + Marian Anderson + Nia |
| 2 | **ONE throughline** | If you can't state message in 1 sentence → quá nhiều |
| 3 | **Michelle must be IN the story** | Her REACTION = emotional anchor. "I put my phone down." |
| 4 | **INHABIT scenes, don't summarize** | "Black dress. Folding chairs. Silence on your chest." > "She went to a funeral." |
| 5 | **Don't pre-sell the story** | No "If I told you..." preambles |
| 6 | **Context-free emails** | Every email = 100% standalone. No "Day 17" references |
| 7 | **Save unused material** | Cut details = future email ideas |
| 8 | **Paste raw customer words** | "bc I just stop to use the restroom" > paraphrasing |
| 9 | **Every scene needs a TURN** | Gap between expectation and reality |
| 18 | **Don't borrow details between emails** | If E1 has spreadsheet moment, E2 cannot |
| 19 | **Opening = event/offer + hook in 3-5 lines** | Cold reader must know what's being sold BEFORE story |

### Voice Rules

| # | Rule | Why |
|---|------|-----|
| 10 | **Segment-specific voice** | Registered SMS ≠ High Engaged SMS ≠ Mass Email |
| 13 | **SMS = friend texting** | No scripted dialogue format ("George: / Me:") |
| 14 | **SMS cadence = one thought per line** | Fragments OK. "lol" > forced emoji |
| 15 | **Scarcity through conversation** | "300 limit and 4,000 people" dropped casually |
| 16 | **SMS jokes must be organic** | Funny situations > funny writing |
| 20 | **SMS teaser ≠ corporate announcement** | "Heyyy" > "Attention." |
| 21 | **Real customer voices > manufactured stories** | Raw quotes with typos = undeniable proof |

### Craft Rules

| # | Rule | Why |
|---|------|-----|
| 11 | **Recurring characters = architecture** | Build arcs across emails. Silence > speech. |
| 12 | **Show the receipt** | "A transfer. Done." Transparency = trust |
| 17 | **Em dash overuse = AI tell** | Max 2-3 per email. Replace with periods, commas |

## 2.3 Idea Sources & Research Pipeline

### 4 Sources

| Source | What to look for | Frequency |
|--------|-----------------|-----------|
| **Customer Reviews** (TrustPilot, Website) | Stories, unexpected uses, emotional reactions | Weekly scan |
| **Customer Emails/DMs** | Raw quotes, complaints, praise, life moments | As received |
| **News + Cultural Calendar** | Current events connecting to brand values | Daily |
| **Reddit + Community** | r/Naturalhair, audience language, shared experiences | Weekly |

### Idea Format

Mỗi ý tưởng cần 5 elements:

```
IDEA: [tên ngắn]
├── Hot Dog (Hook):      Câu chuyện hấp dẫn — reader PHẢI đọc tiếp
├── Broccoli (Product):  Sản phẩm/feature cần push
├── Law (Vehicle):       Dùng Law nào từ Playbook?
├── Mechanism:           Kỹ thuật nào? (Seinfeld, Scene Stacking, etc.)
└── Core Value:          Pride? Resilience? Joy? Heritage? Sisterhood?
```

### Customer Voice Processing Pipeline

```
Receive feedback → Classify (Story/Compliment/Product/Suggestion)
→ Extract quote → Rate potential (1-5⭐) → Tag → Store
→ When ⭐⭐⭐⭐+: Move to "ready to write" queue
```

**Rating criteria:**
- ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ = Has drama, strong emotion, vivid details — READY TO WRITE
- ⭐⭐⭐⭐ = Has specific scene/moment — easy to build story
- ⭐⭐⭐ = Can use as supporting detail
- ⭐⭐ = Nice detail but not dramatic enough
- ⭐ = Simple feedback, hard to story-ify

## 2.4 Subject Line Craft

### Top Patterns

| Pattern | Technique | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| **Curiosity gap** | Statement that demands resolution | "She walked in. They stared." |
| **Pattern interrupt** | Breaks expectation | "Men don't post about handbags..." |
| **Raw emotion** | Visceral, unfiltered | "It HURTS, Mommy!" |
| **Shock/Misdirection** | Starts one way, goes another | "Your Afroyla order: shipped… and REFUNDED?!" |
| **Intimacy** | Private moment shown publicly | ""Mom. Are you okay?"" |
| **Quote format** | Real words in quotation marks | ""I cannot believe or trust anything these days"" |
| **Unexpected teacher** | Someone unlikely gives lesson | "My 7-year-old taught me about sales" |
| **Social tension** | Awkward social situation | "Would it be rude if I bought one?" |
| **Vulnerability** | Founder admits weakness | "I was going to be a writer." |

### Subject Line Rules

1. **Short > long** — under 50 chars ideal
2. **Never reveal the whole story** — create gap
3. **Persona voice** — sounds like Michelle, not marketer
4. **No ALL CAPS warnings** — "🚨 FLASH SALE 🚨" = spam trigger
5. **Preview text ≠ subject line repeat** — add context/follow-up hook

### Preview Text Strategy

| Email Type | Preview Text |
|-----------|-------------|
| DAILY/STORY | Often **blank** (curiosity gap) |
| SALE | Specific context: "170 bags. Starting from $9.95" |
| TEASER | Follow-up hook: "Then my daughter destroyed me at 6:52 AM" |

### Core Themes (recurring across campaigns)

| Theme | How it appears | Best for |
|-------|---------------|----------|
| **Being SEEN** | Bag as mirror, recognition | Customer review emails |
| **Showing up as yourself** | Natural hair, authenticity | Story emails |

| **Overcoming doubt** | Internal monologue about worthiness | Vulnerable stories |
| **Black joy/pride** | Cultural celebration without apology | Season/holiday emails |
| **Family closeness** | George, Nia, personal moments | Scene setters |

## 2.6 The P.S. System

### Two Recurring Formats

**Nia's Daily Fact (educational):**
> **P.S.** Nia told me this morning that on THIS day — February 19th, 1942 — the Tuskegee Airmen were activated. Black men told they weren't smart enough to fly combat planes. She looked at me and said, "They showed up anyway, Mom."

**George Joke (comic relief):**
> **P.P.S.** George heard Tamara's story and said, "I would look GREAT with natural hair." George is bald. 💀

### P.S. Function

1. **Lightens emotional weight** after heavy story
2. **Reinforces family brand** — readers stay for George/Nia
3. **Reward for reading** — "reading to the end" bonus
4. **Sometimes contains secondary CTA** — subtle

### P.S. Rules

- P.S. = **mandatory** for every email
- Use Nia facts for cultural/historical emails
- Use George jokes when story was heavy → need tonal relief
- **Never put selling message in P.S.** of a story email — it cheapens both

## 2.7 Writing Techniques Cheat Sheet

### Sentence Construction

| Technique | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **One-line paragraphs** | "She paused." |
| **ALL CAPS for emphasis** (sparingly) | "That's the LOOK." |
| **Bold for key emotional beats** | **"She showed up as herself."** |
| **Italics for inner voice** | *"it's too soon. They don't know me well enough."* |
| **Em dash for ONE dramatic pause** | "Not a bag — a WITNESS." (max 2-3 per email) |
| **Parenthetical asides** | *(This is going to sound dramatic.)* |

### Anti-Patterns (AVOID)

| ❌ Don't | ✅ Do Instead |
|----------|-------------|
| "If I told you..." preambles | Jump-cut into the scene |
| "Here's the thing" transitions | Trust the structure |
| "So that's the story" lazy bridges | Surface insight plainly |
| Brochure triplets (3 parallel phrases) | One strong statement |
| Sentimental restatements | Let the scene speak |
| "Before I tell you a story..." | The story IS the email |
| --- horizontal rules | Invisible seams (carry-over, timestamp, jump-cut) |

### The "Invisible Seam" Technique

Instead of `---` between sections, use:

| Seam Type | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **Carry-over** | Last word of section 1 = first thought of section 2 |
| **Timestamp** | "It was 9:47 PM." (creates natural break) |
| **Jump-cut** | Abruptly shift scene → reader's brain fills the gap |
| **Echo word** | Repeat one word to bridge sections |
