## IDEAS

### IDEA 1: "George Is Packing Boxes Already" — George chuẩn bị sớm ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Source:** George character arc — E1 he pulled up a chair, E2 he named it, E3 he's reading customer replies → E4/SH1 he's DOING STUFF

**Opening frame:** "heyyy so you know we have March Madness coming up right? Leather from $9.95. Mar 20-22. Private sale. Only for people who register."

**Hot Dog:** Michelle gets home. George is in the garage. He's got tape, boxes, and a label maker he bought from Staples without telling anyone. He's "pre-staging" orders. She tells him: "George, the sale hasn't started yet." He says: "Michelle, preparation is the key to excellence." Did he... did he read that on a poster somewhere? He absolutely did.

**Why it works:** George ESCALATION arc. He went from asking → naming → now PHYSICALLY preparing. In-character. Funny. Shows momentum. Reader sees: even George is getting ready → I should be too.

**CTA:** "So yeah... if GEORGE is ready, maybe you should be too. Reply AFR995E"

---

### IDEA 2: "He Told His Barber" — George spreading the word ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Source:** E2 already says "He's been telling everyone since. Marcus. Steve. Big Ron. His barber. The man at the gas station who did not ask." → This is the BARBER SCENE expanded.

**Opening frame:** "Ok so... you already know about March Madness right? Leather bags & wallets from $9.95. March 20-22. Private sale, register-only."

**Hot Dog:** Michelle finds out George told his BARBER about March Madness. Not casually — like a full sales pitch. The barber texted Michelle: "Your husband just spent twenty minutes telling me about a sale I can't even buy from. He showed me a picture of a wallet. I cut hair, Michelle." George apparently told him "My wife is selling leather wallets for $9.95 and if you don't tell your wife about this I don't know what to tell you." The barber's wife texted AFR995E within the hour.

**Why it works:** George as unofficial sales rep = hilarious. Barber = new character, fresh. Shows word-of-mouth happening organically. "The barber's wife texted AFR995E" = social proof + CTA model in one.

**CTA:** "If a barber's wife from across town is signing up, maybe you should too. Reply AFR995E"

---

### IDEA 3: "I Can't Sleep" — Michelle vulnerability ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Source:** Michelle's recurring "late night scrolling" / "2:43 AM" character trait. Shipping KPI deadline stress.

**Opening frame:** "hey. it's late. you're probably asleep. but I wanted to tell you something about what's coming March 20."

**Hot Dog:** Michelle can't sleep. Sale is 7 days out. She's doing the math in her head. The shipping rate target. How many orders she needs. She keeps opening the calculator app and closing it. George is snoring next to her. She's lying in bed staring at the ceiling thinking: what if nobody comes? Then she opens her phone and sees 500+ people have already registered. For a sale that doesn't start for another week. She puts her phone down. Breathes.

**Why it works:** Vulnerability + insider access. High Engage segment sees the REAL Michelle, not the confident CEO. Creates empathy. "500+ registered" = social proof delivered through emotion, not announcement.

**CTA:** "March 20-22. From $9.95. Reply AFR995E if you haven't yet. I need you there."

---

### IDEA 4: "Marcus Cleared the Warehouse" — Operations scene ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Source:** Marcus character — "texts from warehouse: 'We ready.'" + "Marcus doesn't call unless it's serious."

**Opening frame:** "hey — quick update on March Madness. Leather from $9.95, March 20-22. Private sale."

**Hot Dog:** Marcus texted Michelle this morning. Two words: "We ready." Michelle asks ready for what. Marcus: "The madness." He cleared an entire section of the warehouse. Reorganized everything. Labels on every shelf. He's been doing this for two days and told nobody. When Michelle walked in and saw it she asked: "Marcus, the sale isn't for another week." He almost smiled. (He never smiles.) "I know. But I don't want to be running around on Day 1 like last time."

**Why it works:** Marcus is a loved character. "Almost smiled" = payoff. Shows the OPERATION is real — products are being staged. Grounding detail.

**CTA:** "If Marcus is ready... girl, be ready. Reply AFR995E"

---

### IDEA 5: "Nia Asked Me a Question" — Family moment ⭐⭐⭐

**Source:** Nia character — asks piercing questions, wisdom beyond years, Women's History Month

**Opening frame:** "so we're doing a sale March 20-22 — everything from $9.95. Private, register-only."

**Hot Dog:** Nia overheard Michelle and George talking about March Madness at dinner. She asked: "Mom, why are you selling your bags for $9.95? Don't you always say women should know their worth?" Michelle froze. Because that IS what she always says. And Nia waited with that devastating calm she has. Michelle explains: it's not about the bags being worth less. It's about making sure more women can carry them. Nia thinks about it. "So it's like... opening the door wider?" Yes, baby. That's exactly what it is.

**Why it works:** Nia as moral compass. "Opening the door wider" = campaign philosophy in a child's words. But may be too heavy for SMS format. Better for email.
