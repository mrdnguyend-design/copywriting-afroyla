I made Jennifer read a customer review three times yesterday before I'd let her get back to work.

She kept trying to brush it off.

"Okay, Michelle, I read it—"

"No. Read it AGAIN. Out loud this time."

Kate's at the next desk trying not to laugh.

Because here's what a customer named Jordan wrote on Trustpilot:

“Spectacular Brand led by a PHENOMENAL Team of Black Women Superheroes…. 

Shout-outs to Jennifer & Kate for both being patient, communicative, intentional, and warm with every email message shared between us.” 

Jennifer finishes reading it. Looks up at me.

"That's really nice of him."

"NICE?!" I say. "The man called you SUPERHEROES."

Kate chimes in: "We appreciate it. Jordan was a good customer."

They're both already turning back to their screens.

I'm standing there like... wait, that's IT?

"Hold on," I say. "What did you even DO for Jordan to make him write that?"

Jennifer shrugs. "He had questions about his order. We answered them."

Kate nods. "Yeah. Kept him updated. Nothing crazy."

"That's it?" I ask.

"Yeah," Jennifer says. "Just... normal customer service?"

And that's when it hit me.

What my team thinks is "normal" is what Jordan thinks is "phenomenal."

Because the bar in this industry? It's on the FLOOR.

Most brands ghost you after checkout. Most "customer service" is a bot that sends you in circles.

Most humans behind the screen act like you're bothering them by asking where your $300 bag is. 

So when Jennifer and Kate just... answer emails? Keep people updated? Treat customers like human beings?

Jordan thinks they're superheroes.

But here's the thing, sis:

That "just normal" energy?

It's in everything we do.

It's in customer service. It's in the leather we source. It's in the stitching. It's in the hardware.

We don't know how to do anything halfway.

When you order from Afroyla, you're not getting "good enough."

You're getting the standard that made a grown man write a five-star review praising my team BY NAME.

And that standard doesn’t stop at words. It shows up in what we make, and how we take care of you.
