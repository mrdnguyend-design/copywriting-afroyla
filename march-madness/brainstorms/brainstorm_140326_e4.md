### IDEA 1: "Selection Sunday Is Tomorrow" — George's Bracket vs Michelle's Bracket ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Source:** Selection Sunday = Mar 15, literally TOMORROW from E4's send date (Mar 14). George already obsessed with brackets (E2). NCAA First Round starts same weekend as Afroyla sale.

**Hot Dog:** George is READY for Selection Sunday. He cleared the DVR. Snacks are on the list. Marcus, Steve, and Big Ron are coming over. Michelle is in the other room planning HER bracket — her version of March Madness. George's bracket: 68 teams, one winner. Michelle's bracket: every product on the site, prices so low they'd get eliminated in round one. George's bracket takes 3 weeks. Michelle's takes 3 days. George's bracket = entertainment. Michelle's = leather wallets for $9.95.

**Bridge:** Tomorrow the NCAA reveals their bracket. On March 20, Michelle reveals hers. And in this version, nobody gets eliminated — except the prices.

**P.S.:** George tried to combine the two brackets. He put the Thundara handbag as a 12-seed against Duke. "Michelle, the Thundara is an upset pick." She said George you can't compare a HANDBAG to a basketball team. He said "The Thundara would win. Duke's overrated."

**Subject Lines:**

- "Selection Sunday is tomorrow."
- "George has two brackets now."
- "In my bracket, nobody gets eliminated."

**Why it works:** TIMELY — Selection Sunday literally tomorrow. Builds on George/bracket from E2 organically. NCAA parallel = fun, culturally relevant. Shows the Afroyla sale AS an event, not just a discount.

- "Queens just made the tournament."

- "Nobody predicted this."

**Why it works:** Real NCAA news happening RIGHT NOW. "Queens" = double meaning (Queens University + Afroyla's queens). First-timer narrative = inviting NEW customers. Aspirational.

### IDEA 3: "Alice Counted the Inventory Three Times" — Team Prep Behind the Scenes ⭐⭐⭐⭐

**Source:** Character universe — Alice (accountant/operations) character. Behind-the-scenes prep = proving the sale is REAL and big.

**Hot Dog:** Michelle walked into the office this morning. Alice was already there. Spreadsheet open. Calculator in one hand. Coffee in the other. She's been counting inventory since yesterday. Every wallet. Every pouch. Every makeup bag. Every handbag. Counted them once. Counted them again. Then counted a third time "just to make sure."

Michelle asked: "Alice, are you okay?" Alice looked up. "Michelle, do you know how many individual price tags I have to change? Do you know how many SKUs are going from $140 to $14.95? I need a moment. I need several moments."

But here's what Alice said next, quietly, almost under her breath: "This is going to be the biggest thing we've ever done."

**Bridge:** If Alice — the woman who does breathing exercises during sale days — is saying this is the biggest thing we've ever done... believe her. March Madness. March 20-22.

**P.S.:** George offered to help count inventory. Alice said "George, the last time you helped me count something, you counted the same box twice and told me we had 40 extra wallets." He said "I was being thorough." She said "You were being George."

**Subject Lines:**

- "Alice counted the inventory three times."
- "She said this is the biggest thing we've ever done."
- "Alice needs several moments."

**Why it works:** Alice introduced as narrator = stakes feel REAL. "Breathing exercises" callback = character consistency. Shows SCALE — this sale is serious enough to stress the accountant. George/Alice dynamic = comedy gold per character bible.
