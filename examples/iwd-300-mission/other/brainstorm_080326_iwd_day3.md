## 💡 IDEAS BY TIMESLOT

### 10:00 SMS — Registered (210/300, 90 to go)

**A) "Happy International Women's Day. 90 women to go."**

- IWD morning greeting that's NOT the same generic quote every brand posts.
- "Every other brand is posting a candle emoji today. We're funding coaching."
- Products: Wallet ($19.98), Mini Wallet ($14.98), Eclipse Makeup Bag ($22) = gift-yourself energy.
- "Start with something small. Sometimes self-care starts with a wallet."

**B) "We woke up at 210"**

- Michelle opens dashboard on IWD morning. 210. 70% done.
- "Two days ago this number was zero. This morning I opened my phone and saw 210."
- "Today we finish this."

**C) "The $19.98 IWD gift"**

- Wallets + Makeup Bag = the perfect "I'm buying myself something today" entry point.
- "It's International Women's Day. The United Nations literally said treat yourself. OK they didn't say EXACTLY that but that's how I'm reading it."

---

### 10:00 Mass Email (210/300, 90 to go)

**A) "Happy International Women's Day. Here's what 210 women already did."**

- Differentiator: "You're going to see a lot of IWD posts today. Quotes. Pink graphics. 'Empowerment.' Here's what we did instead: 210 orders. $1,050 toward diabetes prevention coaching. In two days."
- CTA: Text EBOC to 94923.

**B) "The $5 came home" (callback to teaser "What happened to your $5")**

- Full circle: "A few weeks ago I asked what happened to your $5. Today — March 8th — I can tell you: 210 of them showed up. $1,050 toward CYL2. We're 90 away from finishing."
- Warm readers feel the arc. Cold readers get a clear, self-contained story.

**C) "This is what International Women's Day looks like at Afroyla"**

- Not a quote. Not a graphic. 210 orders, $1,050, a real coaching program, a mission that's almost done.
- Visual: numbers, not inspirational fluff.

### 16:00 High Engaged (270-280/300, 20-30 to go)

**A) "George is losing his mind"**

- George watching numbers climb in real time. Pacing. Refreshing the page. Became the mission's unofficial hype man.
- "George is standing behind Tasha's desk WATCHING THE ORDERS COME IN. He's commentating. Like a sports announcer. 'ANOTHER ONE. That's 275. Michelle. MICHELLE. TWENTY-FIVE MORE.'"
- "I said George PLEASE sit down. He said 'I can't sit down we're about to finish.'"
- Direct link (High Engaged pattern).

**B) "20 left. This is personal."**

- Skip the comedy. At 20 remaining, the energy is real.
- "You've been with us. You open everything. You read everything. You're one of the most engaged customers we have."
- "20 spots left. Come close this out with us."
- Direct link.

**C) "George called it"**

- Callback to Day 2 when George said "you finish tomorrow." He was right.
- "Remember when George pulled out his phone calculator and said 'at this rate you finish tomorrow'? It's tomorrow. He was right."
- "Don't EVER tell him I said that."

### 18:00 Registered SMS (290/300, **10 LEFT**)

**A) "10." — THE CLOSER**

- The most important SMS of the entire campaign.
- "10 orders left. TEN. I am not OK right now."
- George full circle: "Two days ago George counted on his fingers and said '185 to go.' Tonight I'm looking at 10."
- "10 women. 10 orders. 10 spots. That's it."
- Products: Handbags + Backpacks back up front = the big finale pieces.

**B) "We're going to finish tonight"**

- "290. I'm looking at 290 orders and I need to say that number out loud because it doesn't feel real."
- "This started as a wild idea — 300 orders, one goal — and we're TEN women away."
- "I don't have a speech. I just have a link and 10 spots."

**C) "The last 10 seats at the table"**

- "There are 300 seats at this table. 290 are filled. 10 are empty."
- IWD energy: a table of women who showed up.
- "It's International Women's Day. And there are 10 seats left. Sit down."

**D) "George is crying" (comedy + emotion finale)**

- "George just looked at the dashboard. 290. He went quiet. He walked to the corner of the warehouse. And I think... I think he's tearing up?"
- "'George are you OK?' 'We're gonna finish it, Michelle.'"
