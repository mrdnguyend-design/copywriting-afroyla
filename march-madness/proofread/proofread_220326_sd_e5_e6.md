## ✉️ EMAIL 1: SD-E5

2. **Missing the Point:** ✅ PASS. Core tension = Michelle keeping a secret for 3 days, which perfectly frames the Crossbody reveal as meaningful, not just another product drop.

3. **Lead Failure (Rule #28):** ✅ PASS. "The last game. Final day. And I'm going ALL IN." — Relevant (it IS the last day), Entertaining (ALL IN energy), Brief (10 words). Hits all three.

4. **Muddled Argument:** ⚠️ MINOR. The email has two arguments: (a) Crossbody reveal + (b) Wallets return. The Wallets section ("And one more thing...") feels slightly bolted-on. It shifts from emotional reveal to factual update mid-stream. Fix: connect the Wallets return to the same emotional arc. "And because you SHOWED UP and ASKED for them..."

5. **Weak Insight:** ✅ PASS (N/A — sale email, not tip email).

6. **No Sales Desire:** ✅ PASS. The desire is built through exclusivity ("I kept this secret"), scarcity ("140 spots"), and urgency ("not promising these last until dinner").

7. **Em Dash Count:** ✅ PASS. **3 em dashes** — all in the product flow paragraph (line 39). Technically 1 over the recommended 2, but they're used structurally in a list-like flow ("$29.95 — brand new", "$9.95 — back because you DEMANDED", "$9.95 — the survivors"). This is functional, not decorative. **Verdict: acceptable, but could tighten to 2 by restructuring one.**

### Additional Checks

- **Anti-Staccato:** ⚠️ CAUTION. Lines 23-33 are all very short sentences. "The last game. Final day." / "Crossbody Bags. From $29.95. Normally $109.95." / "But I wanted the last day to feel different." / "They're on the page right now. Brand new. First time ever at this price." — 11 consecutive short sentences. This is rhythmically correct for a REVEAL MOMENT (staccato builds tension), but adding one medium-length sentence in the middle would vary the rhythm.

- **"Queen" language:** ✅ Used ("queens" in handoff context). Not overused.

- **Logic check:** ✅ All prices correct. Crossbody $29.95 (norm $109.95). Wallets $9.95. Mini Wallets $9.95.

### Fixes Applied

1. **Wallets transition** — connected to the emotional arc: "Because you asked. Multiple times. Some of you messaged me at MIDNIGHT."
2. **One em dash removed** — restructured Mini Wallets line.
3. **One medium sentence added** — breaks the staccato block before CTA.
