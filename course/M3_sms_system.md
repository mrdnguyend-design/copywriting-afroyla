# Module 3: SMS Marketing System

> **Mục tiêu:** Master SMS marketing — the most intimate channel. Michelle texting bạn thân, NOT brand broadcast.
> **Data source:** 10 SMS templates, 40+ SMS files across 3 campaigns, learnings from 6+ rounds of SMS voice iteration.

---

## 3.1 SMS Golden Rules

### 8 Rules Tuyệt Đối

| # | Rule | Vì sao |
|---|------|--------|
| 1 | **Never say "VIP" or "EXCLUSIVE"** | Show exclusivity through BEHAVIOR, not labels |
| 2 | **One joke/angle per SMS** | One killer line > three okay ones |
| 3 | **Lead with relationship, not deal** | "Sis, I did something" > "FLASH SALE 🚨" |
| 4 | **Under 320 chars core message** | Klaviyo SMS sweet spot |
| 5 | **Rotate angles** | Never same angle twice in a row |
| 6 | **Product name + price anchor always** | "[Product]" $X.XX (normally $XX.XX) |
| 7 | **One character max per SMS** | George OR Alice OR no one. Never both. |
| 8 | **End with warmth** | "— Michelle 💜" never "— Afroyla Team" |

### The Voice Test

| ❌ Brand SMS | ✅ Michelle SMS |
|-------------|----------------|
| "FLASH SALE! 50% OFF HANDBAGS! Shop now →" | "Sis. Quick. Between us." |
| "Exclusive VIP offer for you!" | "George doesn't know. Alice DEFINITELY doesn't know." |
| "Don't miss out on this deal!" | "Grab yours and we never speak of this price again. 🤫" |
| "Text INSIDER to join" | "Reply AFR995E to learn more" → "Heyyy" |
| "Attention: Sale starts tomorrow" | "I did something. And I can't take it back lol" |

### SMS ≠ Email Compressed

From the learnings (6+ rounds of SMS voice iteration):

> "SMS = friend texting, not performing comedy. No scripted dialogue format. Michelle shares her day and the joke emerges from the situation."

| Element | Email | SMS |
|---------|-------|-----|
| Length | 500-1000 words | Under 320 chars |
| Structure | Story arc, scenes, pivot | One thought per line, fragments |
| Comedy | Scene-based, building | Situation-based, organic |
| Dialogue | Full character exchanges | NO "George: / Me:" format |
| CTA | Embedded in narrative | Direct link |
| Sign-off | "Love, Michelle" | "— Michelle 💜" or "— M 💜" |
| Emoji | Minimal (👑 and 💜 max) | Natural texting: lol, 😂, 🤫, 😭 |

| 8 | **"The Confessional"** | Vulnerable + funny | Crazy deals, clearance |

| 10 | **"The Story Text"** | Mini narrative | When real story exists |

### Angle Templates (Top 5 Most Used)

#### Angle 3: "George Doesn't Know" (Conspiracy)

```
{{ first_name|default:'Sis' }}. Quick. Between us.

"[PRODUCT]." $[DEAL]. Was $[NORMAL].

George doesn't know. Alice DEFINITELY doesn't know.
The only people who know are you, me, and God.
And God is judging me right now.

👉 [LINK]

Grab yours and we never speak of this price again. 🤫

— Michelle
```

**Variations:**
- "Alice is in a meeting. I have 47 minutes of unsupervised access to the pricing dashboard."
- "George is at the grocery store. I have a 20-minute window. MOVE."

#### Angle 6: "George's Review" (Comedy)

```
{{ first_name|default:'Girl' }}. George just held up the "[PRODUCT]"
and said "[GEORGE QUOTE]."

[MICHELLE'S DEADPAN RESPONSE]

Anyway. $[DEAL]. Normally $[NORMAL]. 24 hours. Just for you.

👉 [LINK]

— Michelle (currently ignoring George) 😂
```

**George quote bank:**
- Wallet: "Can I keep my fishing license in it?" → "George. Go away."
- Tote: "Will it fit my bowling ball?" → "George. That's not— just GO."
- Round bag: "It's shaped like a CIRCLE." → "...yes, George."

#### Angle 9: "The Receipt" (Ultra-Short)

```
{{ first_name|default:'Sis' }}.

"[PRODUCT]"
Was: $[NORMAL]
Now: $[DEAL]
Who: just you
How long: 24 hours

👉 [LINK]

[ONE-LINER]

— M 💜
```

**One-liner options:** "Don't make me beg." / "Tell nobody." / "This is the text."

## 3.3 Segment-Specific SMS Voice

### 3 Distinct Voices

| Segment | Voice | Cadence | Example |
|---------|-------|---------|---------|
| **Registered** | Direct, urgent, Michelle selling | One thought per line, link-heavy | "IT'S LIVE 🏀 Your link: [LINK]" |
| **High Engaged** | Personal, warm, George as bridge | Story-driven, relationship-first | "I know who you are. Not your name — your pattern." |
| **Mass** | Casual teaser, drive-to-register | Short, simple CTA | "Heyyy... bags & wallets from $9.95. Reply AFR995E" |

### Registered SMS Patterns

**Warmup phase (before sale):**
```
WS1: "You're on the list. Here's what's coming."
WS2: Warehouse preview — tagging prices, someone tries to buy early
WS3: Sale page leak — screenshot or MMS
WS4: "Tomorrow 10AM. Be ready."
```

**Sale-day cadence:**
```
Day 1: 10AM (launch) → 2PM (update) → 7PM (recap)
Day 2: 10AM (link) → 6PM ("24 hours left")
Day 3: 10AM (final day) → 6PM ("6 hours") → 9PM ("3 hours")
```

**Product rotation within sale-day SMS:**
```
S1-S2: Premium bags (attention grabbers)
S3-S4: Mid-tier handbags + personal favorites
S5-S6: Wallets + affordable items (objection removal)
S7-S8: "Everything still left" + urgency close
```

### High Engaged SMS Patterns

| # | Content | Tone |
|---|---------|------|
| SH1 | Story teaser — Michelle + price reveal moment | Behind-the-scenes |
| SH2 | George / warehouse / scarcity story | Humor + insider |
| SH3 | Sale day: "[X] sold in first hour" | Excitement, real numbers |
| SH4 | Day 2: "[Item] sold out. Here's what's left." | Urgency |
| SH5 | Final: "Closing tonight. Last chance." | Direct |

**Key insight from IWD campaign:** HE SMS needed 2-3 rewrite rounds because initial drafts were "too performative." The fix:

> "I know who you are. Not your name — your pattern. You're the one who opens every email. Who clicks but doesn't always buy. Who's been watching since before we had matching wallets."

### Mass SMS Patterns

Short. Simple. Reply = register.

```
SM1: "Heyyy... bags & wallets from $9.95. Reply AFR995E to learn more"
SM2: "[X] registered. Spots filling."
SM3: "Tomorrow 10AM. Register now or miss it."
SM4: Day 2 nudge: "Still time. Text AFR995E."
```

## 3.4 SMS Warm-Up Sequences

### The Evolution Pattern (from IWD learnings)

This pattern was learned through 6+ rounds of SMS revision (the hardest content piece to get right):

| # | Days Before | Angle | Example |
|---|-------------|-------|---------|
| 1 | -7 | **Behind-the-scenes** | Michelle tagging $9.95 on bags in warehouse |
| 2 | -5 | **Data anomaly** | Dylan: "500% add-to-cart spike. Zero orders." → They're preloading |
| 3 | -3 | **Packing preview** | Who's packing fastest in warehouse |
| 4 | -1 | **Game day prep** | "Tomorrow 10AM. Set your alarm." |

### What Makes SMS Warm-Up Different from Email Teaser

| | Email Teaser | SMS Warm-Up |
|---|---|---|
| **Structure** | Scene → story → pivot → CTA | One moment → punchline |
| **Comedy** | Constructed scenes, character dialogue | Situation comedy — "someone asked to buy early" |
| **Scarcity** | "180 jackpot bags for 500 people" | "Girl I'm not even done tagging yet lol" |
| **Length** | 500-1000 words | Under 320 chars |
| **Characters** | Multiple per scene | ONE max |

### Case Study: The Warehouse SMS (6+ Iterations)

**What went wrong initially:**
- V1-V3: Comedy-first → scripted George dialogue → "thiếu tự nhiên"
- User feedback: "Không giống tin nhắn giữa bạn bè"

**What fixed it:**
- V4-V6: Friend-chat → situation-based humor
- "Someone asked to buy early" = organic joke + natural scarcity
- "How do I say no nicely lol" = perfect friend-chat
- Rounded prices ($57 not $57.18) = how friends text numbers

> **Golden SMS lesson:** Funny SITUATIONS > funny WRITING. "Someone tried to buy early" is naturally funny. "I'm financially unwell" is constructed comedy.

## 3.5 SMS Quick Reference

### Before Writing Checklist

- [ ] Who's the segment? (Registered / High Engaged / Mass)
- [ ] What angle? (check rotation calendar — don't repeat)
- [ ] Product name + both prices included?
- [ ] Under 320 chars (before link)?
- [ ] Sounds like Michelle texting, or brand announcing?
- [ ] ONE character max?
- [ ] Ends with "— Michelle" + warmth?
- [ ] Read aloud — would you forward this to a friend?
