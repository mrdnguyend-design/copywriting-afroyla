# DAILY | 110426 | What's In My Bag

**Subject:** I've watched over 100 of these videos.
**Preview:** And I finally get it.

{{ person.first_name|default:'love' }},

I've watched over 100 "what's in my bag" videos on TikTok since last night.

*(I was looking for ad ideas for our bags. That's my story and I'm sticking to it.)*

But somewhere around video 40, I stopped looking for ideas. Because I noticed something.

The bigger the bag, the more stuff they carried. Not because they needed it. Because the space was there. So they filled it.

An empty pocket is an invitation. A zipper compartment is a dare. Give a woman a bag with six pockets and she will find six things to put in them, even if five of those things are gum wrappers and a hair tie from 2019.

One woman pulled out a full-size umbrella. In July. Looked at the camera and said "you never know." Ma'am. It is 94 degrees.

Another one had three phone chargers. Three. She said "one for me, one for my man, one for whoever needs it." That woman is running a public utility out of her handbag.

I sat there, cold coffee in hand, and thought: huh.

That's not just a bag thing.

Bigger apartment, more furniture. Bigger calendar, more commitments. Bigger bag, more stuff you didn't even know you were carrying. Not because any of it matters. Because the space was there and we just... filled it.

Then. Video 97 or something.

A woman picked up a tiny crossbody. Put in her phone. Her keys. Zipped it. Walked out the door.

That was the whole video.

And she looked lighter. Not just the bag. Her.

I looked at my bag by the door. The big one. The one with Nia's headband, a sample swatch from Keisha, a granola bar I don't remember buying, and hot sauce from... I don't even want to know.

Maybe the small bags aren't small. Maybe I just never let myself carry less.

So I went to our site. *(Still on the couch. Still in my pajamas. George was making eggs and pretending not to judge me.)*

I pulled up the Mini Satchels. The Small Leather Bags. The Circle Crossbody Bags. Looked at them like I was seeing them for the first time.

Phone. Wallet. Keys. Done. That's all they hold. That's the whole point.

No room for a fork from Chipotle. No room for three chargers. No room for the things you've been carrying around just because you forgot to take them out.

I think these might be my favorite bags we've ever made. And I think they might need to be part of the Mother's Day Sale. April 21-23. I'm still working out the details. But if you want to know before everybody else, text **MOM** to **94923**. Takes 5 seconds.

Love,
Michelle
