Words like BLACK MIRACLE. BLACK IS BEAUTIFUL. We needed to pick six, and we'd been stuck on the sixth spot.

So I put it to you: reply carry if you want it in, skip if you don't.

[X] of you replied carry. Design #6 is in.

This morning, Keisha texted me:

*"Can you thank everyone for me? Tell them I really appreciate their support for my vision."*

I texted back: Keisha. You wanted this bag from the beginning. You told me to keep it.

She said: *"...I know. But now I have [X] reasons."*

I said: I'm telling them you said that.

She said: *"please don't."*

Six leather bags, the kind that walk into a room before you do, and every one of them saying something out loud.

The collection still doesn't have a name — she's still reading through the suggestions you sent in — but these six are locked. Production starts Monday and we're keeping the batch small by design. Launch is June.

I had the production order open on my other screen this morning. There's a field that asks: how many do you want to make? I've been staring at that number since Monday.

The safe move is to keep the number small. I know that. But I've also been watching this inbox the last few days: the replies, the carries, the forwards people sent to their nieces and daughters, one-word notes from women who had never emailed me before.

And that's the problem.

If I make too few bags and they sell out in two days, I guessed wrong. If I make too many and June moves slowly, the leather just sits. Either way, that's on me.

I was looking at that blank number when the thought came:

*What if they just told me first?*

That's what a Pre-Order does.

You tell me you want one before I submit anything Monday. I count the yeses. If enough of you say yes, I know to make more — and you get the bag at a better price than what it'll be when it launches publicly in June. You get it earlier too, before the photos go out, before anyone else sees it on the site.

The number I'm watching is one hundred.

If a hundred of you reply *Y* by Sunday at midnight ET, I'll run a Pre-Order. If we don't get there, I'll make my best guess on Monday and see everyone in June at launch price.

Just reply Y — no link, no form, nothing to fill out.

If you'd rather wait and find them on the site in June, that's a completely fine answer. I just want to know the real number before I commit to one myself.

Love,
Michelle
