Coffee was still brewing. I hadn't had a sip yet.

Nia walked in. Hair everywhere. Socks not matching.

"Mom. You know what I'd tell you every morning if I could?"

I put the spoon down.

"...what, babe?"

"Just Blessed."

I looked at her.

"I was on your phone yesterday. I saw a wallet on your store. It has 'Just Blessed' on it. In gold. I think it's called... Ba-ra-ka-ya? Is that how you say it?"

"That's how you say it."

"I like that one a lot."

Then her hand came out of her pocket. A little wad of bills. Ones, fives, a ten. Held together with one of those tiny rubber bands from the produce section.

She put it on the counter.

"$50."

"Nia. What is this?"

"My money. From the real piggy bank. Not the allowance one."

(She has two. She thinks I don't know about the second one. I love her.)

"Can you bring it home tonight? I want to give it to Grandma for Mother's Day."

Grandma being George's mom.

I opened my mouth to say, "Babe, put your money back, Mommy's got it."

Because that's the script. Right? Kid wants to do something sweet, Mom foots the bill, everybody wins.

Except Nia didn't hand me the idea.

She handed me the MONEY. With the rubber band. From the piggy bank I'm not supposed to know about.

She wasn't asking me to buy it.

She was telling me she already had.

The spoon went in the sink.

"Come here for a sec."

I opened my laptop. Pulled up the secret page I've been building for two weeks — the one Alice keeps pacing behind me about, going "Michelle. The. Margins."

The Early Mother's Day Sale. Apr 21 through 23. Only people on the list get in.

I turned the screen toward Nia. Scrolled to the Barakaya.

"See that price? That's today's. And this one? That's Tuesday's price. For people on the list."

Her eyes got bigger.

"...that's less."

"A lot less."

"How do I get on the list?"

 

Okay. Real quick. I need to pause the story and talk to YOU, because Nia's question is also the question I keep getting all week and I don't want anybody to miss this.

A LOT of you have been hitting "reply" to my emails and typing "MOM" in the reply box.

Babes. Sweet angels of mine. I love you so much. That does NOT register you. My inbox is not the list. Alice is not sitting in my email folder with a clipboard. (Although now I'm picturing it and honestly same vibes.)

Here is what actually works. Exactly this:

1. Pick up your phone.
2. Open the Messages app. The one you text your sister from.
3. Start a new text to this number: 94923
4. Type one word: MOM
5. Hit send.

That's it. Just MOM. Three letters. Not "MOM please" or "MOM for the sale" or "hi Michelle it's LaTasha sign me up MOM." The robot holding this list is very literal. One extra letter and she pretends she never saw you.

You should get a text back within a few seconds. If you didn't, that's your sign to check what you typed. Peek at it. Fix it if it's off. Try again.

Okay. Back to my kitchen.

 

Nia grabbed my phone. Opened Messages. Typed MOM. Hit send.

Confirmation buzzed back in three seconds. She read it out loud like she was narrating a game show.

"I'M ON THE LIST!"

"You are on the list."

"Can you be on it with me?"

"Baby. I literally run the list."

"I know but be on it with me."

So I pulled out my own phone and texted MOM to 94923 like a normal customer. She watched. Nodded once. Satisfied.

"Now we're both on it."

 

There are almost 2,000 of you on that list already.

I want 5,000 before Tuesday morning. That's the real number. Because 5,000 on a private list means the math gets to do a thing the math doesn't usually get to do, and Alice has to stop pacing, and the Barakaya gets to go home to a whole lot of grandmas who deserve it this year.

If my ten-year-old can figure out how to text one word to one number, so can you.

MOM → 94923

That's the whole job. The list closes Tuesday morning.

Love,
Michelle.

P.S. An hour later Nia came back. "Can Grandma be on the list too? So she can get the good prices next year, for herself?" I told her Grandma doesn't need the list. Grandma has us. She gave me a look and I had to turn around and pretend to check the oven.
